<h2>What is Fall?</h2>

Fall is one of the four seasons in the Northern Hemisphere, typically lasting from September to November. The other seasons are winter, spring and summer. During this season, the days become shorter, the nights become longer and the temperature starts to cool down in preparation for winter. 

<h2>When Does Fall Start?</h2>

The exact date of the first day of fall varies from year to year, but in general, it begins on the autumnal equinox, which is the first day of fall in the Northern Hemisphere. The autumnal equinox usually occurs between September 21 and 24, depending on the year. 

<h2>What is the Autumnal Equinox?</h2>

The autumnal equinox marks the moment that the sun is directly over the equator, and it is the point when day and night are of equal length. After this date, the days become shorter and the nights become longer as the sun moves southward.
