<h2>What Are Turkey Vultures?</h2>

Turkey vultures (Cathartes aura) are large birds of prey found in North America. They have a wingspan of up to 72 inches, and weigh up to six pounds. Turkey vultures are dark brown or black in color with a bald head and a hooked bill. They have excellent eyesight, and are adept at locating food from the air.

<h2>Where Do Turkey Vultures Live?</h2>

Turkey vultures are found throughout the United States, Canada, Mexico and Central America. They prefer to inhabit open areas such as fields and pastures, but can also be found in wooded areas and even urban settings.

<h2>Do Turkey Vultures Pose a Threat to Cats and Dogs?</h2>

No, turkey vultures pose no threat to cats and dogs. They do not hunt live animals and will not attack them. However, they may be attracted to dead animals, which could pose a danger if the animal has been poisoned or is diseased.

<h2>Are Turkey Vultures Protected?</h2>

Yes, turkey vultures are protected by the Migratory Bird Treaty Act of 1918. It is illegal to hunt, trap, or kill them.
