What is the Distance to Travel Around the World? 



It's a question that has been asked by travelers and aspiring adventurers for centuries: what is the total distance that must be traveled to circumnavigate the world? The answer depends on a few variables, including the route taken and the type of transportation used. In this article, we'll explore the different methods of travel and estimate the total distance for a full circumnavigation.

<h2>How Long Is the Equator?</h2>

The equator is the imaginary line that divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. It is approximately 24,901 miles long and its circumference can be used to approximate the total distance of a round-the-world trip.

<h2>What Is the Shortest Distance Around the World?</h2>

The shortest distance around the world is the great-circle route. This route follows the circumference of the Earth, taking a straight line from point to point. It is the shortest distance between any two points on the globe, and it is approximately 24,859 miles long.

<h2>What Is the Longest Distance Around the World?</h2>

The longest route around the world is the rhumb line. This route follows a straight line on a Mercator projection map, taking a zig-zag course across the globe. It is the longest distance between any two points on the globe, and it is approximately 25,006 miles long.

<h2>What Is a Typical Route Around the World?</h2>

Most travelers opt for a typical route that combines elements of the great-circle route and the rhumb line.
