<h2>What Is Radiation?</h2>

<p>Radiation is a type of energy that is emitted from a source. It is made up of tiny particles or waves, which travel through space and may be absorbed by other objects. Radiation can be classified into two types; ionizing radiation and non-ionizing radiation. Ionizing radiation consists of high-energy particles, such as gamma rays, X-rays, and alpha particles, while non-ionizing radiation consists of low-energy particles, such as infrared radiation and microwaves.</p>

<h2>Types of Radiation and Their Speeds</h2>

<p>The speed at which radiation travels depends on its type.  Gamma rays, which are composed of high-energy particles, travel at the speed of light, which is the fastest speed possible. X-rays, which are also composed of high-energy particles, travel slightly slower than gamma rays, at about 99.8% of the speed of light. Alpha particles, which are composed of medium-energy particles, travel at about one-tenth the speed of light. Infrared radiation, which is composed of low-energy particles, travels at about one-millionth the speed of light. Microwaves, which are composed of very low-energy particles, travel at about one-billionth the speed of light.</p>

<h2>How Does Radiation Travel Through Space?</h2>

<p>Radiation travels through space in waves or particles. These waves or particles are emitted from a source, such as a star or a planet. As they travel through space, they can be absorbed by other objects. For example, when a planet absorbs radiation from a star, it heats up. This is known as the greenhouse effect. The speed at which the radiation travels depends on its type, with some forms of radiation travelling faster than others.</p>

<p>Gamma rays are the fastest form of radiation that travels through space, travelling at the speed of light. X-rays travel slightly slower than gamma rays, at about 99.8% of the speed of light.
