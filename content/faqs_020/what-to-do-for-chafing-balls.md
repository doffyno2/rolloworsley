<h2>What Causes Chafing Balls?</h2>
Chafing balls are caused by the rubbing of skin against skin, clothing, or other materials. This can occur when the skin is too dry and does not have enough lubrication to prevent the rubbing. This type of friction is common in the groin area and can be caused by tight clothing, sweating, and excessive activity.

<h2>How to Prevent Chafing Balls</h2>
The best way to prevent chafing balls is to keep the skin lubricated. This can be done by using a lubricant such as petroleum jelly or baby oil. Applying a light layer of lubricant before engaging in any activity can help prevent chafing. Additionally, wearing loose-fitting clothing that is made of breathable fabrics can help reduce friction.
