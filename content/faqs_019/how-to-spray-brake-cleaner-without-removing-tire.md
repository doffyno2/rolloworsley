<p>In order to keep your car running safely and efficiently, it is important to keep your brakes in good condition. Fortunately, it is possible to do so with a few simple steps.</p>

<p>Before spraying brake cleaner, make sure that the car is parked on a level surface and the brakes are off. If the brakes are still on, the brake cleaner can cause damage to the brake components.</p>

<p>Before you begin, make sure you have the following items:</p>

<ul>

<h2>Steps to Spray Brake Cleaner Without Removing Tire</h2>

<p>The following steps will help you spray brake cleaner without removing the tire.</p>

<ol>
    <li>Raise the car with the floor jack and secure it with jack stands. Make sure the car is securely supported before you begin working.</li>
    <li>Remove the brake dust shields and/or caliper brackets. This will allow you to access the brakes and spray the cleaner directly onto the brake components.</li>
    <li>Put on your protective gloves and goggles or face shield.</li>
    <li>Spray the brake cleaner onto the brake components. Make sure to spray all of the components including the calipers, rotors, and drums.</li>
    <li>Allow the cleaner to sit on the components for a few minutes, then wipe off the excess with a rag or paper towel.</li>
    <li>Reinstall the brake dust shields and/or caliper brackets.</li>
    <li>Lower the car from the jack stands and remove them.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Safety Tips for Spraying Brake Cleaner</h2>

<p>When spraying brake cleaner, it is important to take safety precautions to avoid potential injury or damage. Here are some tips for safely spraying brake cleaner:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Always wear protective gloves and goggles or a face shield when spraying brake cleaner.</li>
    <li>Make sure the car is securely supported before you begin working.</li>
    <li>Keep the brake cleaner away from open flames or sparks.</li>
    <li>Do not spray brake cleaner onto hot components.</li>
    <li>Do not allow the brake cleaner to come in contact with skin, eyes, or clothing.</li>
</ul>
