<p>If you own a Kenmore stove, you may have noticed the error code “F10” appearing on its display. This code can be quite alarming, as it indicates a potentially dangerous situation. In this article, we’ll explain what it means when your Kenmore stove says F10, what you should do if you see it, and how to prevent it from happening again.</p>

<h2>What Does F10 Mean?</h2>

<p>F10 is an error code that indicates a runaway temperature in the oven. In other words, the oven’s temperature is higher than it should be, and it is not responding to the temperature settings you’ve selected. This can be caused by a malfunctioning oven sensor, a faulty control board, or a thermostat that is out of calibration.</p>

<p>A runaway temperature can be dangerous, as it can cause the oven’s internal components to overheat and potentially cause a fire. For this reason, it’s important to address the issue as soon as you see the F10 code.</p>

<h2>What Should I Do When My Kenmore Stove Says F10?</h2>

<p>If your Kenmore stove displays an F10 code, you should take the following steps:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Turn off the oven and unplug it from the power outlet.</li>
    <li>Check the oven’s temperature sensor and ensure that it is properly connected.</li>
    <li>Check the control board for any signs of damage or corrosion.</li>
    <li>Check the thermostat and make sure that it is calibrated correctly.</li>
    <li>If the issue persists, contact a professional oven repair technician for assistance.</li>
</ul>

<h2>How Do I Prevent My Oven From Showing the F10 Error Code?</h2>

<p>There are several steps you can take to reduce the risk of your oven displaying the F10 error code:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Clean and inspect the oven regularly.</li>
    <li>Check the temperature sensor and make sure it is connected properly.</li>
    <li>Check the control board and replace it if necessary.</li>
    <li>Calibrate the oven’s thermostat regularly.</li>
    <li>If you notice any unusual behavior, contact a professional oven repair technician immediately.</li>
</ul>
