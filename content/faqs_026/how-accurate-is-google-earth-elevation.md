<p>The accuracy of the satellite imagery used by Google Earth varies depending on the type of satellite used. Generally, the higher resolution imagery is more accurate. <h2>2. Digital Elevation Model Accuracy</h2>

<p>Google Earth also uses Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) to create its maps. DEMs are a digital representation of the land surface and are often used to create topographic maps. Google Earth uses DEMs with a resolution of up to 30 meters, which means that the elevation data provided is accurate to within 30 meters.</p>

<p>The accuracy of Google Earth also depends on the location of the user. In areas with poor GPS reception, Google Earth may not be able to accurately locate the user.
