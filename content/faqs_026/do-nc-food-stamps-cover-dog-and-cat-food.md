<h2>What Are Food Stamps?</h2>

<p>Food stamps, also known as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), are government benefits that provide money to purchase food. SNAP was created to help those living in poverty to afford nutritious meals. The program is funded by the US Department of Agriculture (USDA).</p>

<p>Food stamps are available to qualifying individuals and families who meet certain income and asset criteria. In North Carolina, the program is administered by the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS). Eligible households receive a monthly benefit amount, which can be used to purchase food at authorized retail stores, farmers markets, and other food outlets. The amount of benefits a household receives depends on the size of the household and their income.</p>

<h2>What Can Food Stamps Be Used For?</h2>

<p>Food stamps can be used to purchase most types of food and food products. This includes items such as bread, cereal, fruits, vegetables, and dairy products. Food stamps can also be used to purchase non-food items such as paper products and household cleaning supplies. However, food stamps cannot be used to purchase alcohol, tobacco, pet food, vitamins, or medicines.</p>
