<h2>Why Fish Species Names Are Capitalized</h2>

<p>In formal writing, fish species names are capitalized because they are classified as proper nouns. Proper nouns are the names of specific people, places, and things, and are typically capitalized. This includes scientific and common names of fish species. </p>

<p>Fish species names are also capitalized to make them easier to read and remember. Capitalizing the names of these organisms makes them stand out from the rest of the text and allows them to be easily recognized and understood. </p>

<p>When referring to a specific fish species, it is important to use the correct common name and scientific name. For example, the scientific name for the common carp is Cyprinus carpio, while the scientific name for the Atlantic salmon is Salmo salar. </p>
