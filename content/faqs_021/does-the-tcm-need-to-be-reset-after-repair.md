<p>If you drive a vehicle that has an automatic transmission, you may have heard that the transmission control module (TCM) needs to be reset after a repair. In short, yes, the TCM does need to be reset after a repair or service.</p>

<p>The TCM is the computer that controls the shift points and other functions of the transmission. It is responsible for monitoring the performance of the transmission and adjusting the shift points and other functions as needed. When a repair or service is performed on the transmission, the TCM needs to be reset so that it can properly monitor and adjust the shift points and other functions. If the TCM is not reset, then it will not be able to properly monitor and adjust the shift points and other functions, resulting in poor performance.</p>

<p>In order to reset the TCM, you need to access the diagnostic port on the vehicle. This is usually located under the dashboard or in the engine compartment. Once the diagnostic port is located, then you can connect a scan tool to the port and reset the TCM.
