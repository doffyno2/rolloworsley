<h2>What is a Repo Company?</h2>

<p>A repo company, also known as a repossession company, is a business that specializes in recovering vehicles that have fallen behind on payments or have been purchased with fraudulent means. Repo companies are hired by creditors, such as banks and auto finance companies, to locate and repossess vehicles to recover the unpaid balance. Repo companies are legally allowed to enter private property to repossess a vehicle and are usually bonded and insured to protect the creditor from any damage that may occur during the repossession.  </p>

<h2>How Do Repo Companies Find Vehicles?</h2>

<p>Repo companies use a variety of tactics to locate and repossess vehicles, including:</p>

<h3>Skiptracing</h3>

<p>Skiptracing is the process of finding a person's current whereabouts by analyzing information from public databases, credit reports, and other sources. Repo companies use skiptracing to locate the registered owner of a vehicle and determine their current address. Once the repo company has the owner's current address, they can then send a letter to the owner informing them of the repossession and arrange a time to pick up the vehicle.  </p>

<h3>GPS Tracking</h3>

<p>GPS tracking is used by repo companies to monitor the location of a vehicle and determine when it is in an accessible location. GPS tracking devices are usually installed on a vehicle without the owner's knowledge, allowing the repo company to track the vehicle's location in real time. When the repo company determines that the vehicle is in an accessible location, they can then quickly move in and repossess the vehicle. </p>

<h3>Surveillance</h3>

<p>Surveillance is another tactic used by repo companies to locate and repossess vehicles. Repo companies may hire private investigators or send their own agents to monitor and observe a vehicle's location. Surveillance allows repo companies to determine when and where a vehicle is located and can be a very effective way of locating a vehicle that is not responding to other methods. </p>

<h3>Social Media</h3>

<p>Social media is increasingly being used by repo companies to locate and repossess vehicles. Repo companies can use social media to track a vehicle's location and determine when it is in an accessible location. Repo companies can also use social media to locate the registered owner of a vehicle and send them a message informing them of the repossession.  </p>
