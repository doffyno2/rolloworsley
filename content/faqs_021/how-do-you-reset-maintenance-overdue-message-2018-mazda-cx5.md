<p>Are you the proud owner of a 2018 Mazda CX-5? If so, you may have encountered a message saying that maintenance is overdue. This message can be reset with a few simple steps. Read on to find out how to do it.</p>

<h2>What is the Maintenance Overdue Message?</h2>

<p>The maintenance overdue message is a reminder to owners of the 2018 Mazda CX-5 that their vehicle needs to have its regular maintenance done. This message can appear on the dashboard, in the infotainment system, or both. It is important to reset this message after maintenance has been done, as it can be a source of distraction and annoyance.</p>

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Overdue Message</h2>

<p>The process for resetting the maintenance overdue message on a 2018 Mazda CX-5 is fairly straightforward. Follow these steps to reset the message:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Turn on your Mazda CX-5</h3>

<p>The first step is to turn on your 2018 Mazda CX-5. Make sure that the car is in park or neutral and the parking brake is engaged.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Access the Maintenance Menu</h3>

<p>Once the car is on, press the “Settings” button on the dashboard. This will bring up a menu of options, including the “Maintenance” option. Select this option.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Select “Reset Maintenance”</h3>

<p>Once you are in the Maintenance menu, you will be able to select the “Reset Maintenance” option. Select this option.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Confirm Reset</h3>

<p>Once you have selected the “Reset Maintenance” option, you will be asked to confirm the reset. Select “Yes” to confirm the reset.</p>

<h3>Step 5: Exit the Menu</h3>

<p>Once the reset is complete, you can exit the Maintenance menu by pressing the “Settings” button again. The maintenance overdue message should now be reset.</p>
