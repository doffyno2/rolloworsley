<h2>What is a Filter?</h2>
A filter is an important part of any aquarium, as it helps keep the water clean and clear. It draws water from the tank and runs it through a series of sponges, gravel, and other materials, which trap debris and waste. The filtered water is then returned to the tank. There are a variety of types of filters available, including canister filters, hang-on-back filters, and undergravel filters.

<h2>Can Betta Fish Get Stuck in Filter?</h2>
Unfortunately, betta fish can sometimes get stuck in a filter. This is especially true of hang-on-back filters and undergravel filters. Betta fish are curious and love to explore, and they may find their way into the filter intake tube. Once they get in, they may be unable to swim back out, due to the powerful water suction. This can cause the fish to become stressed and eventually die.

<h2>How to Prevent Betta Fish from Getting Stuck in Filter?</h2>
Fortunately, there are a few steps you can take to prevent your betta fish from getting stuck in a filter. The most important thing is to make sure the filter intake is covered with a filter guard or sponge. This will help prevent the fish from swimming into the intake tube. You should also inspect the filter regularly to make sure it is functioning properly. Finally, you should make sure you are using the proper filter size for your tank.
