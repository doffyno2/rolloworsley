<h2>What Is the Menstrual Cycle?</h2>

<p>The menstrual cycle is the process by which a woman's body prepares for pregnancy. The cycle typically lasts between 24 and 38 days, and is divided into two phases. The first phase is the follicular phase, which begins with the first day of the menstrual period and ends with ovulation. During this phase, the lining of the uterus thickens and hormones like estrogen and progesterone are released. The second phase is the luteal phase, which begins after ovulation and ends with the start of a new menstrual period. During this phase, the uterus prepares to receive a fertilized egg and the thickened uterine lining is shed if no pregnancy occurs. </p>

During the follicular phase, oxytocin stimulates the pituitary gland to release two other hormones, luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH).
