<p>Gum can be an appealing snack for dogs, but it can also be a very dangerous one. Ingesting gum can cause a range of gastrointestinal problems, and can even lead to death if not treated quickly. If you ever find that your pup has eaten gum, it's important to act fast and get them to the vet as soon as possible.</p>

<h2>Signs and Symptoms of Gum Poisoning in Dogs</h2>

<p>If your dog has eaten gum, you’ll want to watch for signs of gum poisoning. Common symptoms include:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Vomiting</li>
  <li>Diarrhea</li>
  <li>Lethargy</li>
  <li>Lack of appetite</li>
  <li>Abdominal pain</li>
  <li>Upset stomach</li>
  <li>Dehydration</li>
</ul>

<p>These symptoms can be mild or severe, depending on how much gum your pup has eaten. If you notice any of these signs, it’s important to get your pup to the vet right away.</p>

<h2>Treatment for Gum Poisoning in Dogs</h2>

<p>When your pup gets to the vet, they will be able to assess the situation and determine the best course of action. The treatment for gum poisoning will depend on the amount of gum ingested and the symptoms your pup is experiencing. Treatment could include:</p>

<ul>
  <li>IV fluids to treat dehydration</li>
  <li>X-rays to check for foreign objects</li>
  <li>Antibiotics to treat bacterial infections</li>
  <li>Surgery to remove foreign objects</li>
</ul>

<p>In some cases, the vet may be able to induce vomiting to get rid of the gum before it has a chance to cause any further damage. If your pup has eaten a large amount of gum, the vet may recommend surgery to remove it.</p>

<h2>How to Prevent Gum Poisoning in Dogs</h2>

<p>The best way to prevent gum poisoning in dogs is to keep gum out of their reach. Make sure that gum is stored in a secure container, and that it is not within your pup’s reach. Additionally, it’s important to supervise your pup when they are outside or in areas where gum is likely to be. Finally, make sure to keep your pup’s food and treats in a secure area, and to never feed them gum.</p>
