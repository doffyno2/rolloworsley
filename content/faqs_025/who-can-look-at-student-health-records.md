As a student, your health records are a highly sensitive document that must be kept confidential. In order for your records to remain private and secure, it's important to understand who has the right to access them. In this article, we'll discuss who can look at student health records and the circumstances in which they can do so.

<h2> What Are Student Health Records? </h2>

Student health records are documents that contain information about a student's health and medical history. This includes medical conditions, allergies, treatments, and medications. These records are kept confidential and are only accessible to certain individuals. 

<h2> Who Can Access Student Health Records? </h2>

Only authorized individuals have the right to view student health records. These individuals include: 

<h3> School Administrators </h3>

School administrators, such as principals, school nurses, and counselors, have the right to access student health records in order to provide appropriate care and support. 

<h3> Medical Professionals </h3>

Medical professionals, such as doctors, nurses, and therapists, have the right to access student health records in order to provide medical care. 

<h3> Parent or Legal Guardian </h3>

Parents or legal guardians of the student have the right to access student health records in order to ensure that their child is receiving proper medical care. 

<h3> Student </h3>

The student has the right to access their own health records in order to understand their health and medical history. 

<h2> When Can Student Health Records be Accessed? </h2>

Student health records can only be accessed when it is necessary to provide medical care or support to the student. In most cases, the student or their parent or legal guardian must give written consent for the records to be accessed. 

<h2> Who Cannot Access Student Health Records? </h2>

Student health records cannot be accessed by anyone other than the individuals mentioned above. This includes employers, insurance companies, and other third parties. 

<h2> How Are Student Health Records Protected? </h2>

Student health records are protected by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). This law ensures that student health records remain confidential and secure. 

<h2> What Are the Consequences of Unauthorized Access? </h2>

Unauthorized access to student health records can result in criminal charges, including fines and even jail time. It's important to understand who is authorized to access student health records and that only those individuals have the right to view them.
