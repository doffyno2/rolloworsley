The Russian Twist is a popular core exercise used to target the abdominals, obliques, and lower back. It is a dynamic exercise that involves both rotation and lateral flexion of the spine. It is a great way to strengthen the core and improve balance and stability.

<h2>How to Do the Russian Twist</h2>

The Russian Twist is performed by sitting with your feet on the ground and your knees bent. Your back should be kept straight as you lean back slightly with your arms outstretched in front of you. From this position, you rotate your torso to the left and then to the right. As you do this, your arms should remain outstretched and your feet should remain in the same position. You should feel your abdominal muscles and obliques working as you rotate.

<h2>Benefits of the Russian Twist</h2>

The Russian Twist is a great exercise for strengthening the core and improving balance and stability. It also helps to improve posture as it works the muscles in the mid-back. The Russian Twist can be done with or without weights and can be performed at different levels of intensity for different fitness goals.

<h2>Muscles Targeted by the Russian Twist</h2>

The Russian Twist is a great exercise for targeting the abdominals, obliques, and lower back. It works the rectus abdominis, the external obliques, and the erector spinae. Additionally, it can help to strengthen the hip flexors and hamstrings.

<h2>Variations of the Russian Twist</h2>

The Russian Twist can be done with or without weights. It can also be performed with a medicine ball, a kettlebell, or a weight plate. Additionally, there are variations that involve twisting the torso to the left and right at different angles.

<h2>Safety Considerations for the Russian Twist</h2>

The Russian Twist is a great exercise for strengthening and toning the core but should be performed with caution. Make sure to keep your back in a neutral position to avoid straining the lower back. Additionally, keep the movement slow and controlled and avoid jerking motions.
