<h2>What Is Ranch Dressing?</h2>

<p>Ranch dressing is a type of salad dressing made from mayonnaise, buttermilk, garlic, onion, and other spices. It originated in the United States in the 1920s, and has since become a popular condiment worldwide. It is also used as a dip for vegetables, chips, and other snacks.</p>

<h2>Nutritional Information</h2>

<p>So, how many calories in 2 tablespoons of ranch dressing? The exact amount depends on the brand and type of ranch dressing, but generally speaking, two tablespoons of ranch dressing contain around 100-130 calories. This is in comparison to blue cheese dressing, which contains around 135-150 calories in two tablespoons.</p>

<p>In addition to calories, ranch dressing also contains fat, carbohydrates, and protein. Two tablespoons of the dressing contains around 10-12 grams of fat, 1-2 grams of carbohydrates, and 1-2 grams of protein. As with any condiment, ranch dressing should be consumed in moderation. If you’re trying to watch your diet, be mindful of how much ranch dressing you use.</p>

<h2>Health Benefits Of Ranch Dressing</h2>

<p>Despite being high in fat and calories, ranch dressing can actually be beneficial for your health. It contains healthy fats from the mayonnaise, which can help reduce inflammation in the body. It also provides small amounts of important vitamins and minerals, such as Vitamin A, Vitamin E, and calcium. Eating foods with ranch dressing can also help you get your daily dose of dietary fiber and protein.</p>

<p>In addition, ranch dressing can be a great way to add flavor to a salad or sandwich. Instead of adding sugar or high-calorie sauces, use ranch dressing as a way to add flavor and nutrition to your meal.</p>

<h2>How To Make Ranch Dressing</h2>

<p>If you’d like to make your own ranch dressing at home, it’s actually quite simple. All you need is mayonnaise, buttermilk, garlic, onion, and herbs. Simply combine all the ingredients in a blender and blend until the mixture is creamy and smooth. You can also add other flavors and spices to customize the flavor of your ranch dressing.</p>
