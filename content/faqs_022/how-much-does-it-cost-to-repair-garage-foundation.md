<h2>What Causes Garage Foundation Damage?</h2>

Garage foundations can become damaged for a variety of reasons. The most common cause of damage is water. If the garage is located in an area that is prone to flooding or if there is a lot of rain or snow, water can seep into the foundation and cause it to crack or weaken. 

In addition to water, garage foundations can also be damaged by tree roots, shifting soil, poor construction, and even vibrations from nearby traffic or construction. If any of these causes of damage are present, it’s important to address them as soon as possible to avoid further damage. 

<h2>Types of Garage Foundation Repairs</h2>

The type of repair that is needed for a garage foundation depends on the extent of the damage. Minor damage, such as small cracks, can often be repaired with caulking or epoxy. Major damage, however, may require more involved repairs, such as reinforcing the foundation with steel beams or replacing the entire foundation.
