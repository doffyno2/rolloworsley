<h2>What is a Skyscraper?</h2>

A skyscraper is a tall, multi-story building that rises above the skyline of a city. The term “skyscraper” was first used in the late 19th century to describe tall buildings in New York City. Today, the term is used to describe any tall building, regardless of where it is located. Generally, a building must be more than 150 meters (500 feet) tall to be considered a skyscraper.

<h2>The Tallest Skyscrapers in the World</h2>

The Burj Khalifa is currently the world's tallest skyscraper, standing 828 meters (2,717 feet) tall. Located in Dubai, the Burj Khalifa was completed in 2010 and is the centerpiece of the Dubai skyline. It is made up of 163 floors and was designed by renowned architect Adrian Smith.

The Shanghai Tower is the second tallest skyscraper in the world, standing at 632 meters (2,073 feet) tall. Located in Shanghai, the Shanghai Tower was completed in 2015 and is the tallest building in China. It has 121 floors and was designed to be a multi-functional building, with offices, retail stores, and even a hotel.

<h2>What is a Mile-High Tower?</h2>

A mile-high tower is a structure, typically a skyscraper, that is built to reach a height of one mile (1.6 kilometers). While there are currently no buildings that reach this height, there have been several proposals for mile-high towers. The most ambitious proposal is the Mile High Tower, which is a proposed 1,609-meter (5,280 feet) tall structure in Saudi Arabia.

<h2>How Many Floors in a Mile?</h2>

The exact number of floors in a mile-high tower depends on the height of each floor. Generally, a floor is considered to be around 2.4 meters (8 feet) tall. Using this measurement, a mile-high tower would have around 656 floors. However, in some cases, floors can be as tall as 3.7 meters (12 feet), which means that a mile-high tower would have around 432 floors.
