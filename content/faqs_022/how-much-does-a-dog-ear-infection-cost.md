<p>Ear infections in dogs can be caused by a variety of factors, including allergies, poor hygiene, ear mites, foreign objects in the ear, and infections caused by bacteria or yeast. <h2>What Are the Signs and Symptoms of a Dog Ear Infection?</h2>

<p>The most common signs and symptoms of a dog ear infection include itching, redness, odor, discharge, and head shaking. Your dog may also paw at their ears or have difficulty hearing. If your pet is exhibiting any of these symptoms, they should be seen by a veterinarian as soon as possible.</p>

<h2>How Is a Dog Ear Infection Diagnosed?</h2>

<p>In order to diagnose a dog ear infection, your veterinarian will perform a thorough physical examination of your pet’s ears. This will include looking for visible signs of infection, such as redness, swelling, or discharge. Your vet may also use an otoscope to look into your pet’s ear canal for any foreign objects or debris. In some cases, your vet may take a sample of the discharge from your pet’s ear and send it to a lab for analysis.</p>

<h2>What Are the Treatment Options for a Dog Ear Infection?</h2>

<p>Treatment for a dog ear infection will depend on the underlying cause. In some cases, your veterinarian may prescribe antibiotic ear drops or oral medications to treat the infection. In other cases, your vet may recommend a procedure such as ear flushing or cleaning to remove any foreign objects or debris. In some cases, your vet may recommend surgery to remove any growths or tumors in the ear.</p>
