<h2>The Basics of Brake Pads</h2>

Brake pads are made of a combination of metal and friction materials, and they sit between the brake caliper and the brake rotor. When the brakes are applied, the brake pads press against the rotor, creating friction which slows the car down. Over time, the friction material on the brake pads wears down, and they must be replaced in order to maintain a safe driving experience.

<h2>Replacing Brake Pads</h2>

Once you have determined the size of the brake pads for your car, you can begin the replacement process. To replace the brake pads, you will need to remove the caliper from the rotor, and then remove the old brake pads from the caliper. Once the old brake pads are removed, you can install the new ones and reattach the caliper to the rotor.
