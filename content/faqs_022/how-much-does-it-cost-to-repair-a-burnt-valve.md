<h2>What Causes Burnt Valves?</h2>

<p>Valves can become burnt for a variety of reasons. The most common cause is an incorrect valve timing, which can cause the valves to open and close too quickly. This can generate a lot of heat, which can damage the valves. Other causes include a worn valve guide, a worn valve seat, or a worn cam lobe. Poor timing can also be caused by worn components such as spark plugs, timing belts, or camshafts.</p>

<h2>How Is A Burnt Valve Diagnosed?</h2>

<p>When a burnt valve is suspected, the first step is to perform a compression test. This test will determine the pressure in each cylinder of the engine, and can help to identify which cylinder is having a problem. In addition, the mechanic will inspect the valves and the valve seats to determine if there is any damage. He or she may also use a visual inspection, which involves looking for signs of burning or scorching.</p>
