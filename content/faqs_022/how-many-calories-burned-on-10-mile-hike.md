<h2>How Many Calories Does an Average Person Burn on a 10 Mile Hike?</h2>

The answer to this question depends on the individual and the type of terrain they are hiking on. <h2>Factors That Influence Calories Burned on a 10 Mile Hike</h2>

There are a number of factors that influence the amount of calories a person will burn when they go on a 10 mile hike. One of the most important factors is the weight of the person. A person who weighs more will burn more calories, as their body is working harder to move. Age is also a factor, as older people tend to burn fewer calories than younger people.

The type of terrain a person is hiking on is also a factor. If a person is hiking on flat terrain, they will burn fewer calories than if they were to hike on a hilly or mountainous terrain. This is because the body will have to work harder to move up and down hills, thus burning more calories.

The speed at which a person is hiking will also affect the number of calories they burn. If a person is walking at a leisurely pace, they will burn fewer calories than if they were to hike at a faster pace.
