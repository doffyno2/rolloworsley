How Long Do Grits Last?

Grits are a Southern staple that have been around for centuries. They are made from ground corn, and are a popular breakfast food. Grits can be served with butter and cheese, in a casserole, or even as a side dish. But how long do grits last, and how should they be stored? In this article, we'll discuss the shelf life of grits, how to tell when they've gone bad, and how to store them properly.

<h2>What Is the Shelf Life of Grits?</h2>
The shelf life of grits depends on how they are stored. Uncooked grits will last for up to two years if stored in an air-tight container in a cool, dry place. If uncooked grits are stored in the refrigerator, they will last for up to four months.

Cooked grits, on the other hand, will only last in the refrigerator for two to three days. If they are frozen, cooked grits will last for up to two months.

<h2>How Can You Tell if Grits Have Gone Bad?</h2>
When it comes to uncooked grits, it's important to look for signs of mold or any other discoloration. If you see any of these signs, discard the grits immediately.

Cooked grits should be discarded if they have a sour smell, if they appear to be discolored, or if they have a slimy texture.

<h2>How to Store Grits Properly</h2>
Uncooked grits should be stored in an air-tight container in a cool, dry place. If you plan to store them in the refrigerator, be sure to use an air-tight container to prevent them from absorbing any odors.

Cooked grits should be stored in the refrigerator in an air-tight container. If you plan to freeze them, be sure to wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil.
