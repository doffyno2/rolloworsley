<h2>What Is a Rubber Band Welt?</h2>

A rubber band welt is an injury that is caused by something that has a tight grip on the skin. The injury occurs when the tight grip is suddenly released, causing an abrupt and painful stretching of the skin. Rubber bands are a common source of rubber band welts, but they can also be caused by jewelry, clothing, straps, or even just a tight hug. 

<h2>How Long Do Rubber Band Welts Last?</h2>

The length of time that a rubber band welt will last will depend on the severity of the injury. Generally, rubber band welts will last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks. In some cases, the welt may last even longer, up to a few months. 

<h2>What Causes Rubber Band Welts?</h2>

The most common cause of rubber band welts is an abrupt release of tight grip on the skin. This can happen when a rubber band is stretched too far, when clothing or jewelry is too tight, or when straps or belts are pulled too tightly. <h2>How to Care for Rubber Band Welts</h2>

The best way to care for rubber band welts is to keep the area clean and dry. Clean the area gently with a mild soap and water. Applying a cold compress to the area can help reduce swelling and pain. Additionally, over-the-counter medications such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen can also be taken to help reduce pain and swelling.
