<h2>What is a Joist?</h2>

<p>A joist is a horizontal support beam that runs from one wall to the other, typically made from wood or steel. Joists are essential for providing stability and structure for the floors, walls and roofs of any building. Joists are typically installed in a pattern, with each one spaced 12 or 16 inches apart. When joists are compromised or damaged, they must be repaired in order to restore structural integrity to the building.</p>

<h2>What Causes Joists to Become Damaged?</h2>

<p>Joists can become damaged due to a variety of reasons, including excessive weight, poor construction, or age-related wear and tear. Excessive weight can cause the joists to crack or break, while poor construction can lead to weak or uneven joists. Age-related wear and tear can cause joists to become compromised, leading to potentially dangerous structural damage. It’s important to inspect your joists regularly to ensure they are in good condition.</p>
