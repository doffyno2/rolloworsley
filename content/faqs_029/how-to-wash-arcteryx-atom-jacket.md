<p>An Arcteryx Atom jacket is a warm, lightweight jacket designed to keep you warm and comfortable in cold weather. Here’s how to properly wash an Arcteryx Atom jacket.</p>

<h2>Check the Care Tag</h2>

<p>Before you start cleaning your Arcteryx Atom jacket, it’s important to check the care tag. This will indicate whether you should hand wash or machine wash your jacket. If the tag states that the jacket should be machine washed, you can proceed with the cleaning process. If the tag states that it should be hand washed, then you should follow the instructions carefully.</p>

<h2>Gather Your Supplies</h2>

<p>Once you’ve checked the care tag, it’s time to gather your supplies. You’ll need a mild detergent, a few towels, and a large bucket or sink. You’ll also need some rubber gloves if you plan to hand wash the jacket.</p>

<h2>Prepare the Jacket for Cleaning</h2>

<p>Before you begin cleaning your Arcteryx Atom jacket, it’s important to unzip all of the zippers, remove any detachable parts, and turn the jacket inside out. This will help ensure that all dirt and debris is removed from the jacket.</p>

<h2>Machine Wash the Jacket</h2>

<p>If you’re machine washing your Arcteryx Atom jacket, it’s important to use a gentle cycle and a mild detergent. When the cycle is finished, remove the jacket and hang it up to dry. Do not put the jacket in the dryer, as this could damage the fabric.</p>

<h2>Hand Wash the Jacket</h2>

<p>If you’ve determined that your Arcteryx Atom jacket should be hand washed, fill a large bucket or sink with lukewarm water. Add a few drops of mild detergent and gently stir the water. Submerge the jacket in the water and let it soak for about 10 minutes. After the jacket has soaked, gently agitate the fabric to help remove dirt and debris. When you’re finished, rinse the jacket in clean water and hang it up to dry.</p>

<h2>Dry the Jacket</h2>

<p>Once you’ve finished washing your Arcteryx Atom jacket, it’s important to dry it properly. If you’re machine washing the jacket, hang it up to dry on a clothes hanger. If you’re hand washing the jacket, hang it up to dry on a drying rack or clothesline. Do not use a dryer, as this could damage the fabric.</p>
