Deciding whether to position a stove higher or lower than a countertop is a crucial decision that can determine the look and functionality of your kitchen. Stoves are often placed in the middle of a kitchen, and the countertops are placed around it, so it's important to consider the practical implications of both options. <h2>The Pros of a Higher Stove</h2>

Positioning a stove higher than the countertop has a number of advantages. Firstly, a higher stove makes it easier to reach ingredients stored on the shelves above the stove. This can be especially helpful if you have a lot of cooking supplies stored on the shelves. Secondly, a higher stove is safer for young children, as they won't be able to reach the hot surfaces. Finally, a higher stove also looks more aesthetically pleasing, as it creates a more balanced look in the kitchen. 

<h2>The Cons of a Higher Stove</h2>

Although a higher stove has its advantages, there are a few potential drawbacks to consider. Firstly, a higher stove can be difficult to clean, as it's harder to reach the surfaces and corners. Secondly, a higher stove may require the use of a step ladder or stool to reach the surfaces, which can be inconvenient for some people. Finally, a higher stove can take up more space in the kitchen, as it extends further into the room. 

<h2>The Pros of a Lower Stove</h2>

Positioning a stove lower than the countertop also has its advantages. Firstly, a lower stove is easier to clean, as you can reach all the surfaces without having to use a step ladder or stool. Secondly, a lower stove takes up less space, as it doesn't extend as far into the room. Finally, a lower stove can be safer for young children, as they won't be able to reach the hot surfaces as easily.
