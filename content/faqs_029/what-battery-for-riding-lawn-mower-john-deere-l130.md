<h2>Types of Batteries for John Deere L130</h2>

<p>The John Deere L130 riding lawn mower runs on a 12-volt battery. There are three main types of batteries available for the L130: lead-acid, gel, and absorbed glass mat (AGM). Lead-acid batteries are the most common type and are usually the least expensive. They are also the most durable and have a long life expectancy. However, they do require regular maintenance and can be prone to leaking if not properly maintained. Gel batteries are a good option if you’re looking for a battery with a long life expectancy and low maintenance requirements. AGM batteries are the most expensive but are also the most reliable and require the least amount of maintenance.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Battery for Your L130</h2>

If you use your mower often and need a battery with a long life expectancy, you may want to invest in an AGM battery. However, if you only use your mower occasionally and need a battery with a lower cost and lower maintenance requirements, you may want to go with a lead-acid or gel battery.</p>
