<h2>How to Adjust the Height on a Craftsman Riding Mower</h2>

Adjusting the height on a Craftsman riding mower is easy. Here's how to do it:

<h3>Step 1: Park the Mower</h3>

The first step is to park the mower on level ground. Make sure to engage the parking brake and turn off the engine.

<h3>Step 2: Release the Height Adjustment Lever</h3>

Once the mower is parked, release the height adjustment lever. The lever is located on the right side of the mower, near the rear wheel.

<h3>Step 3: Raise or Lower the Height Adjustment Lever</h3>

Once the lever is released, you can raise or lower the cutting height. To raise the cutting height, turn the lever clockwise. To lower the cutting height, turn the lever counterclockwise.

<h3>Step 4: Check the Height</h3>

Once the height adjustment lever is released, you can check the cutting height to make sure it is set correctly. To do this, measure the distance between the ground and the bottom of the mower deck. The desired cutting height should be between 1 and 4 inches.

<h3>Step 5: Engage the Height Adjustment Lever</h3>

Once the desired cutting height is reached, engage the height adjustment lever. To engage the lever, turn it clockwise until it is securely in place.
