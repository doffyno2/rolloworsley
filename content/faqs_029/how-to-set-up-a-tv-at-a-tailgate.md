The first step in setting up a TV at a tailgate is choosing the right TV. There are a few things to consider when choosing a TV for tailgating: size, portability, and power. 

<h3>Size</h3>

When it comes to size, bigger is better. The larger the screen, the more people can enjoy the game or show. It's best to get the biggest TV that you can afford and that will fit in your vehicle. 

<h3>Portability</h3>

Another important factor is portability. You'll need to be able to transport your TV to and from the tailgating location, so it's important to choose a TV that's light enough to be carried. Larger TVs can be cumbersome, so look for one that has a built-in handle or carrying strap. 

<h3>Power</h3>

Finally, consider the power requirements of the TV. If you plan on using an AC power source, then you'll need to make sure the TV has an AC power cord. If you'll be using a DC power source, then you'll need to make sure the TV has a DC power cord.
