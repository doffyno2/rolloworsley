<h2>What Happens When You Pull a Dog's Ears?</h2>

When you pull a dog's ears, it can cause them pain and discomfort. Even if the dog doesn't react, it can still be painful for them. Pulling on a dog's ears can also damage their hearing, as the ear canal is delicate and can be easily damaged. In extreme cases, it can even cause hearing loss.

<h2>Why Do People Pull a Dog's Ears?</h2>

There are a few reasons why people might pull a dog's ears. Some people do it out of curiosity, wanting to see how the dog will react. Others might do it as a form of discipline, thinking it will help teach the dog a lesson. But in most cases, it's done out of ignorance or in an attempt to get the dog's attention. 

<h2>How Can You Stop Your Dog from Pulling Its Ears?</h2>

The best way to prevent your dog from pulling its ears is to train them not to do it. You can do this by using positive reinforcement techniques such as treats or praise when they don't pull their ears. If you catch your dog pulling their ears, you should also firmly tell them "no" and redirect their attention to something else.
