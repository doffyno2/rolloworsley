<h2>Pomeranian Growth Chart</h2>

Pomeranians are considered to be fully grown when they reach around 8-10 months of age. This is when they've reached their adult height and weight. Typically, a Pomeranian will weigh between 3-7 pounds and stand between 6-7 inches tall.

<h2>Factors That Affect Pomeranian Growth</h2>

There are several factors that can affect how quickly or slowly a Pomeranian grows. These include the following:

<h3>Genetics</h3>

The genetic makeup of a Pomeranian can influence its growth rate. Dogs that have larger parents are likely to grow faster and reach their full size earlier than those with smaller parents.

<h3>Nutrition</h3>

<h3>Exercise</h3>

<h2>Signs That A Pomeranian Has Stopped Growing</h2>

Once a Pomeranian has reached its adult size, there are several signs that can indicate that it has stopped growing. These include:

<h3>No More Weight Gain</h3>

If your Pomeranian is no longer gaining weight, then it is likely that it has stopped growing.

<h3>No More Height Increase</h3>

If your Pomeranian is no longer getting taller then it has most likely reached its adult size.

<h3>Physical Changes</h3>

If your Pomeranian's coat and fur are becoming thicker and longer, then it is likely that it has stopped growing.
