What Does The “Y” In Shoe Size Mean?

When it comes to finding the right shoes for your feet, it can be confusing to know what size to purchase. Not only do sizes vary between brands, but there can also be a lot of unfamiliar symbols and letters thrown into the mix. One of the most common symbols you’ll see on shoe sizes is the letter “Y.” So, what does the “Y” in shoe size mean?

When you see the letter “Y” in shoe sizes, it usually means that the shoes are designed for youth or children. The “Y” stands for “Youth” and indicates that the shoes are made for younger feet. Because children’s feet grow at a different pace than adults’, their shoe sizes will be labeled differently. While adult sizes typically range from size 6 to size 12, youth sizes typically start at size 3 and go up to size 7.

It’s important to note that not all shoe brands label their youth sizes with the letter “Y.” Some brands may use a “C” instead, while others may use a different symbol altogether. It’s important to check the size chart of the brand you’re buying in order to determine which symbol stands for youth sizes.
