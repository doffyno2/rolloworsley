What are the Characteristics of Hunting and Gathering Societies?

Hunting and gathering societies are societies in which members rely on wild plants and animals for their subsistence. Hunting and gathering societies have been around for thousands of years and continue to exist in some parts of the world today. These societies have distinct characteristics that help them to survive and thrive in their environment. In this article, we will explore the common characteristics of hunting and gathering societies, what makes them unique, and why they have been able to survive for so long. 



A hunting and gathering society is a type of nomadic society which relies on the practice of hunting and gathering for its subsistence. Hunting and gathering societies live in small, mobile groups and are usually composed of extended families. These societies generally have a hunter-gatherer culture, which is based on the use of plants and animals found in their environment. Hunting and gathering societies do not produce their own food, but rather, they rely on the resources found in their environment.

<h2>Small and Mobile Groups</h2>

Hunting and gathering societies often live in small, mobile groups, ranging from 5 to 20 individuals. These groups are usually composed of extended families and are not tied to any particular location. The small size of the group helps to ensure that the resources available to them are not over-utilized. 

<h2>Hunter-Gatherer Culture</h2>

Hunting and gathering societies have a hunter-gatherer culture, which is based on the use of plants and animals found in their environment. They rely on the resources of the environment for their subsistence, and do not produce their own food. These societies often practice sustainable hunting and gathering, in order to ensure that the resources they rely on will not be depleted. 

<h2>Simple Technology</h2>

Hunting and gathering societies rely on simple technology in order to hunt and gather. They often use simple tools such as spears, nets, and bows and arrows in order to hunt and gather. These tools are usually made from materials found in their environment, such as wood and stone. 

<h2>Division of Labor</h2>

Hunting and gathering societies often have a division of labor, with men responsible for the hunting and women responsible for the gathering. This division of labor helps to ensure that the group is able to efficiently use the resources available to them. 

<h2>Nomadic Lifestyle</h2>

Hunting and gathering societies often have a nomadic lifestyle. These societies are not tied to any particular location, but rather move from place to place in order to take advantage of seasonal resources. This nomadic lifestyle helps to ensure that the group is able to find the resources they need.
