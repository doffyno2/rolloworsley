<h2>Check the Color and Smell</h2>

<p>One of the easiest ways to tell if your sausage is bad is to check the color and smell. Fresh Italian sausage should be a light pinkish-brown color, and it should have a pleasant, meaty smell. If the sausage is discolored, particularly if it has a greenish tint, it is likely spoiled. Additionally, if the sausage has a sour or rancid smell, it is best to discard it.</p>

<h2>Check the Texture</h2>

<p>Another way to tell if your Italian sausage is bad is to examine the texture. Fresh sausage should have a firm, moist texture. If the sausage is slimy or sticky, it is likely spoiled. Additionally, if the sausage has developed a hard or dry exterior, it is best to discard it.</p>

<h2>Check the Package</h2>

<p>Finally, it is important to check the packaging of the sausage for any signs of spoilage. If the package is puffed up or torn, it indicates that the sausage may have gone bad. Additionally, if the package is leaking, it is best to discard the sausage.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>How long can Italian sausage stay in the fridge?</h3>

<p>Italian sausage can stay in the fridge for up to two days. If it is not consumed within that time frame, it should be discarded.</p>

<h3>Can I freeze Italian sausage?</h3>

<p>Yes, Italian sausage can be frozen for up to three months. To ensure it stays fresh, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil.</p>

<h3>Can I tell if Italian sausage is bad by taste?</h3>

<p>Yes, Italian sausage that has gone bad will usually have a sour or rancid taste. If the sausage tastes off, it is best to discard it.</p>

<p>Eating bad Italian sausage can lead to food poisoning, which can cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and fever. If you have any of these symptoms after eating Italian sausage, it is best to seek medical attention.</p>
