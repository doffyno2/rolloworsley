Leather gloves are a staple of winter wardrobe. They provide warmth and protection from the elements. But leather gloves can become stiff and uncomfortable over time. Fortunately, there are a few simple steps you can take to soften them back up and get them looking and feeling like new.

<h2>What You Need to Soften Leather Gloves</h2>

To soften leather gloves, you'll need a few basic supplies:

<ul>
	<li>Leather gloves</li>
	<li>Leather conditioner or oil</li>
	<li>Soft cloth or brush</li>
	<li>Bowl or container</li>
	<li>Vaseline or petroleum jelly</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Soften Leather Gloves</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Clean the Gloves</h3>

The first step in softening leather gloves is to clean them. Use a soft cloth or brush to remove any dirt or debris from the gloves. Make sure the gloves are completely dry before moving on to the next step.

<h3>Step 2: Apply Leather Conditioner or Oil</h3>

Next, apply a small amount of leather conditioner or oil to the gloves. Use a soft cloth or brush to spread the conditioner or oil evenly across the surface of the gloves. Allow the conditioner or oil to soak into the leather for at least 30 minutes.

<h3>Step 3: Wipe Off Excess Conditioner or Oil</h3>

After the conditioner or oil has had time to soak into the leather, use a soft cloth to wipe off any excess. Make sure to wipe off all of the conditioner or oil so that it does not leave a greasy residue on the gloves.

<h3>Step 4: Soften With Vaseline or Petroleum Jelly</h3>

The next step in softening leather gloves is to apply a small amount of Vaseline or petroleum jelly to the gloves. Use a soft cloth or brush to spread the Vaseline or petroleum jelly evenly over the gloves. Allow the Vaseline or petroleum jelly to sit for at least 30 minutes before wiping off any excess.

<h3>Step 5: Polish the Gloves</h3>

The final step is to polish the gloves. Use a soft cloth or brush to buff the gloves to a shine. This will help to maintain the softness of the leather and keep the gloves looking like new.
