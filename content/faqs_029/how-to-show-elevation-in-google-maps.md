Google Maps is a powerful and convenient tool for getting directions and exploring the world. It is also useful for seeing the elevation of different locations, which can be helpful when planning a route with hills or mountains. In this article, we’ll show you how to view elevation in Google Maps and provide some tips for getting the most out of this feature.

<h2>What is Elevation?</h2>

Elevation is a measurement of a point on the Earth's surface relative to sea level. In other words, it is the height of a location above or below sea level. Elevation is an important factor in many activities, including hiking, backpacking, skiing, and construction. Knowing the elevation of a location can help you plan a route, anticipate changes in weather, or calculate the slope of a hill or mountain.

<h2>How to View Elevation in Google Maps</h2>

Google Maps makes it easy to view elevation data in a few simple steps.

<h3>Step 1: Search for a Location</h3>

First, go to Google Maps and search for the location you want to view the elevation of. You can enter a street address, city, or landmark. Once you have entered your search, Google Maps will display the location and a map of the surrounding area.

<h3>Step 2: Select the Elevation Tool</h3>

Once you have located the desired location, you can select the “Elevation” tool from the menu on the left side of the screen.

<h3>Step 3: View the Elevation Data</h3>

After selecting the Elevation tool, Google Maps will display a graph showing the elevation data for the location. The graph will show the elevation in both meters and feet.

<h2>Tips for Using Google Maps Elevation Tool</h2>

The Google Maps Elevation tool can be a helpful resource for planning a route, determining the slope of a hill or mountain, or simply exploring the world. Here are some tips for getting the most out of this useful tool.

<h3>View Multiple Locations</h3>

You can view the elevation of multiple locations by selecting the “Path” tool from the menu on the left side of the screen. Once you have selected the Path tool, you can click on multiple points on the map to create a path and view the elevation data for each point.

<h3>View Elevation Profiles</h3>

You can view an elevation profile of a path by selecting the “Profile” tool from the menu on the left side of the screen. Once you have selected the Profile tool, you can click on multiple points on the map to create a path and view an elevation profile of the path. The elevation profile will show the elevation data for each point in the path.

<h3>View Elevation in 3D</h3>

You can view the elevation of a location in 3D by selecting the “Earth” tool from the menu on the left side of the screen. Once you have selected the Earth tool, you can click on the location and view the elevation in 3D.
