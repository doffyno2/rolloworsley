<h2>What Is a Choke and Throttle?</h2>

<p>The choke and throttle are two separate parts of the engine that work together to control the engine speed. The choke is a valve that restricts the airflow into the carburetor, which lowers the engine speed. The throttle, on the other hand, is a valve that increases the airflow, which raises the engine speed. The choke and throttle are usually located near the carburetor and can be easily accessed for adjustments.</p>

<h2>How to Use Choke and Throttle on Riding Mowers</h2>

<p>When starting a riding mower, the choke should be set in the “on” position. This restricts the airflow, which makes it easier for the engine to start. When the engine is running, the choke should be slowly adjusted back to the “off” position. This will allow more air to flow into the carburetor and will raise the engine speed.</p>

<p>The throttle can be used to further adjust the engine speed. When the throttle is in the “on” position, more air is allowed to flow into the carburetor and the engine will run faster. When the throttle is in the “off” position, the engine will run slower. It is important to note that the throttle should never be set to the “off” position when the engine is running, as this could cause the engine to shut off.</p>

<p>When adjusting the choke and throttle, it is important to adjust them slowly and carefully. If the adjustments are made too quickly, the engine could shut off or become damaged. It is also important to keep an eye on the engine temperature. If the engine becomes too hot, it could cause serious damage.</p>
