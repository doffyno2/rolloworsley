<p>Rust is a common problem for vehicle owners. It occurs when metal is exposed to moisture and oxygen, causing it to corrode. <h2>What Causes Fender Rust?</h2>

<p>Fender rust is caused by metal being exposed to moisture and oxygen. This can happen when metal is exposed to rain, snow, salt, or other chemicals. Rust can also form on metal that is not exposed to the elements but is instead exposed to high humidity or a damp environment. Rust can also form when metal is not properly lubricated or sealed. Rust can spread quickly and can cause serious damage if it’s not taken care of. </p>

<h2>What Are the Signs of Fender Rust?</h2>

<p>Fender rust is usually easy to spot. It will appear as a rusty spot on the metal, often in the form of patches or streaks. The rust will be red or orange in color and will have a rough texture. It may also be accompanied by other signs, such as flaking paint or bubbling beneath the paint. It’s important to address fender rust as soon as it is noticed, as it can spread quickly and cause more damage. </p>

<h2>How Is Fender Rust Repaired?</h2>

<p>Fender rust can be repaired in a few different ways. The first step is to remove the rust. This can be done with a chemical rust remover or by sanding the rust away. Once the rust has been removed, the area needs to be cleaned and treated with a rust inhibitor. Once the rust inhibitor has dried, the area should be sealed with a rust-proof sealant. Finally, the area should be painted with a rust-proof paint to protect it from future rust. </p>
