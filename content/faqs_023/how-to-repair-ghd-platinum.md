How to Repair a GHD Platinum



In order to keep your GHD Platinum hair styling tools running in optimal condition, it is important to repair them regularly. Doing so will ensure that the tool is functioning properly, and will help to extend its life. The following guide will help you to repair a GHD Platinum tool quickly and easily.

<h2>Tools and Supplies Needed</h2>

Before you begin repairing your GHD Platinum, you will need to have the following tools and supplies on hand:

<ul>
	<li>A Phillips head screwdriver</li>
	<li>GHD Platinum repair kit</li>
	<li>Isopropyl alcohol</li>
	<li>Cotton swabs</li>
	<li>Fine-grit sandpaper</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step 1: Clean Out the Device</h2>

The first step to repairing a GHD Platinum is to clean out the device. You can do this by using a Phillips head screwdriver to remove the screws located on the base of the device. Once the screws are removed, you can gently pull apart the two halves of the device and clean the interior using a cotton swab and isopropyl alcohol.

<h2>Step 2: Replace the Heating Element</h2>

The next step is to replace the heating element. To do this, you will need to open up the device and locate the heating element. Once you have located the heating element, you can use a pair of tweezers to remove it. Once it is removed, you can replace it with a new one.

<h2>Step 3: Replace the Power Cord</h2>

The power cord is an important part of the GHD Platinum, and it needs to be replaced if it is damaged. To do this, you will need to open up the device and remove the damaged cord. Once it has been removed, you can insert the new power cord and secure it in place.

<h2>Step 4: Clean the Exterior of the Device</h2>

Once you have replaced all of the internal components, you can use a damp cloth to clean the exterior of the device. This will help to remove any dirt or debris that may have accumulated on the outside of the device.

<h2>Step 5: Apply a Protective Coating</h2>

Finally, you can apply a protective coating to the exterior of the device. This will help to protect the device from scratches and corrosion. You can use a fine-grit sandpaper to lightly sand the exterior of the device, and then use a cloth to apply a protective coating.
