<p>Gas stoves are a popular choice for many people, especially those who need to cook frequently and quickly. The burners on a gas stove are adjustable, allowing you to adjust the heat of the burner to the desired temperature. Adjusting the burners on a GE gas stove is a fairly simple process, and can be done in just a few steps.</p>

<h2>Tools You Will Need</h2>

<p>Before you start adjusting the burners on your GE gas stove, you will need the following tools:</p>

<ul>
  <li>A flathead screwdriver</li>
  <li>A Phillips head screwdriver</li>
  <li>An adjustable wrench</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps for Adjusting the Burners</h2>

<p>Once you have gathered the necessary tools, you can begin adjusting the burners on your GE gas stove.</p>

<h3>Step 1: Remove the Burner Knobs</h3>

<p>The first step is to remove the burner knobs. To do this, take the flathead screwdriver and carefully pry the knobs off of the stove. Once the knobs are removed, set them aside in a safe place.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Remove the Burner Rings</h3>

<p>The next step is to remove the burner rings. To do this, take the Phillips head screwdriver and unscrew the screws that hold the burner rings in place. Once the screws are removed, the burner rings can be lifted off the stove.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Adjust the Burner Valves</h3>

<p>The final step is to adjust the burner valves. To do this, take the adjustable wrench and turn the valves to the desired temperature. Make sure to adjust the valves in small increments to avoid overshooting the desired temperature.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Replace the Burner Rings and Knobs</h3>

<p>Once you have adjusted the burner valves, replace the burner rings and knobs. To do this, take the Phillips head screwdriver and screw the burner rings back into place. Then, take the flathead screwdriver and carefully attach the burner knobs back onto the stove.</p>
