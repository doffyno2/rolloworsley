<p>If you own a 2013 Toyota Tacoma, you may have seen the maintenance light come on. This light indicates that it's time for routine maintenance, such as oil changes or air filter replacements. It's important to reset the maintenance light after each service so that it can keep track of when maintenance is due. Fortunately, resetting the light is a simple process.</p>

<h2>Checking the Maintenance Light</h2>

<p>Before you begin the reset process, you need to make sure that the maintenance light is actually on. To do this, start the engine and then press and hold the trip reset button. This is usually a small button located on the left side of the instrument panel. After a few seconds, the maintenance light will appear in the odometer display.</p>

<h2>Resetting the Maintenance Light</h2>

<p>Once you've confirmed that the maintenance light is on, you can begin the reset process. To do this, turn the ignition key to the "On" position and then press and hold the trip reset button for 10 seconds. After 10 seconds, the maintenance light will turn off. This indicates that the light has been successfully reset.</p>

<h2>Additional Maintenance Tips</h2>

<p>It's important to reset the maintenance light after every service, but it's also essential to stay on top of regular maintenance. To ensure that your 2013 Toyota Tacoma is running properly, it's recommended that you have the oil changed every 5,000 miles and the air filter replaced every 10,000 miles. Additionally, you should have the brakes checked every 15,000 miles and the spark plugs replaced every 30,000 miles.</p>
