<h2>What Causes Steam Damage on Kitchen Cabinets?</h2>

<p>Steam damage on kitchen cabinets is caused by exposure to high levels of moisture and heat. Steam from boiling water, hot food, and even steam cleaners can cause wood to warp and discolor. This type of damage is usually most noticeable on the edges and corners of the cabinets, where steam is more likely to accumulate.</p>

<h2>How to Repair Steam Damage on Kitchen Cabinets</h2>

<p>Repairing steam damage on kitchen cabinets requires a few simple steps. First, you'll need to identify the source of the steam damage. This can be done by inspecting the area for signs of warping, discoloration, and other damage. Once the source has been identified, you'll need to take steps to remove the steam damage.</p>

<h3>Step One: Clean the Area</h3>

<p>The first step in repairing steam damage on kitchen cabinets is to clean the area. Use a soft cloth and mild soap to remove any dirt and debris. Once the area is clean, you'll be able to better see the extent of the damage.</p>

<h3>Step Two: Sand the Area</h3>

<p>Once the area is clean, you'll need to sand the area to remove any remaining dirt and debris. Use a fine-grit sandpaper to lightly sand the area. This will help to smooth out any rough edges and prepare the surface for repair.</p>

<h3>Step Three: Apply Wood Filler</h3>

<p>Once the area is sanded, you'll need to apply wood filler to repair any cracks and gaps. Use a putty knife to apply a thick layer of wood filler to the damaged area. Allow the wood filler to dry completely before proceeding to the next step.</p>

<h3>Step Four: Stain and Seal the Area</h3>

<p>Once the wood filler has dried, you'll need to stain and seal the area. Use a wood stain that matches the color of your cabinets and apply it with a foam brush. Once the stain is dry, apply a clear sealer to protect the area from future damage.</p>
