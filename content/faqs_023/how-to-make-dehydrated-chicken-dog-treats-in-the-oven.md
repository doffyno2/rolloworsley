<p>Treats are an important part of any dog's diet. But store-bought treats can be expensive and not always the healthiest option. Making your own dehydrated chicken dog treats in the oven is a great way to give your pup a yummy and nutritious snack. This simple process doesn’t take long and can be done with a few simple ingredients. </p>

<h2>Ingredients</h2>

<p>To make dehydrated chicken dog treats in the oven, you will need the following ingredients:</p>

<ul>
  <li>2 cups of cooked chicken, broken into small pieces</li>
  <li>1/2 cup of wheat germ</li>
  <li>1/4 cup of olive oil</li>
  <li>1/4 cup of water</li>
</ul>

<h2>Instructions</h2>

<p>Making these delicious treats is simple and can be done in just a few steps:</p>

<ol>
  <li>Preheat your oven to 200 degrees Fahrenheit.</li>
  <li>In a large bowl, mix together the cooked chicken, wheat germ, olive oil, and water until everything is combined.</li>
  <li>Spread the mixture onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 1 1/2 hours.</li>
  <li>Remove the baking sheet from the oven and let cool.</li>
  <li>Cut the treats into small pieces and store in an airtight container in the refrigerator.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Benefits of Making Dehydrated Chicken Dog Treats in the Oven</h2>

<p>Making dehydrated chicken dog treats in the oven is a great way to give your pup a nutritious, homemade treat. Not only are these treats a delicious treat for your pup, but they are also a healthier option than store-bought treats. These treats are full of protein from the chicken, and the wheat germ provides your pup with essential vitamins and minerals. The olive oil provides your pup with healthy fats, and the water helps keep the treats moist. </p>

<h2>Tips for Making Dehydrated Chicken Dog Treats in the Oven</h2>

<p>When making these homemade treats, it’s important to keep a few things in mind:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Be sure to use cooked chicken, as raw chicken can contain harmful bacteria.</li>
  <li>When cutting the treats into small pieces, be sure to keep them small enough for your pup to eat comfortably.</li>
  <li>Store the treats in an airtight container in the refrigerator to keep them fresh.</li>
</ul>
