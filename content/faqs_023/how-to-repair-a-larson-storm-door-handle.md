<p>Larson storm door handles are well known for their durability and long-lasting performance. Unfortunately, even the best-built door handles can eventually wear out, break or become damaged from excessive use. If you’ve noticed that your Larson storm door handle isn’t performing as well as it used to, it’s time to take action and repair it. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to repair a Larson storm door handle.</p>

<h2>Step 1: Inspect Your Door Handle</h2>

<p>The first step in repairing your Larson storm door handle is to inspect it and identify the problem. Start by looking for any visible signs of damage, such as cracks or broken pieces. Also, check to see if any of the screws are loose or missing. If you find any of these issues, they will need to be addressed before you can repair the handle.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Gather the Necessary Tools and Materials</h2>

<p>Once you’ve identified the problem, you’ll need to gather the necessary tools and materials. You’ll need a screwdriver, a drill, some screws, and some lubricant. You may also need to buy some replacement parts, depending on the extent of the damage.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Remove the Existing Handle</h2>

<p>The next step is to remove the existing handle from the door. Start by unscrewing the screws that are holding the handle in place. Once the screws are removed, carefully pull the handle away from the door. If it’s stuck, you may need to use a drill to loosen it.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Clean and Lubricate the Handle</h2>

<p>Once the handle is removed, you’ll need to clean and lubricate it. Start by wiping down the handle with a damp cloth, then apply a small amount of lubricant to any moving parts. This will help ensure that the handle will move smoothly when it is reinstalled.</p>

<h2>Step 5: Replace Any Broken or Missing Parts</h2>

<p>If you noticed any broken or missing parts when inspecting the door handle, now is the time to replace them. Depending on the type of handle you have, the replacement parts may be available at your local hardware store. If not, you may need to order them online.</p>

<h2>Step 6: Reinstall the Handle</h2>

<p>Once you’ve replaced any broken or missing parts, you can reinstall the handle. Start by placing the handle back onto the door, then screw the screws back in place. Make sure the handle is securely attached before moving on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Step 7: Test the Handle</h2>

<p>The final step is to test the handle to make sure it is working correctly. Open and close the door several times to make sure the handle is functioning properly. If it seems to be working correctly, you’re done!</p>
