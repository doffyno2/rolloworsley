<p>Small scratches on LCD and LED computer monitors can be easily fixed at home with a few common items. Whether the scratches are caused by a drop, a pet, or a key, they can often be repaired in a few simple steps. With the right materials and some patience, it is possible to make your monitor look as good as new.</p>

<h2>What You'll Need</h2>

<p>To repair small monitor scratches, you will need rubbing alcohol, cotton swabs or a soft cloth, and a cleaning solution. Rubbing alcohol can typically be purchased at a local drug store or supermarket. Cotton swabs or a soft cloth can be found at most hardware stores. A cleaning solution can be purchased from any electronics store or online.</p>

<h2>Step One: Clean the Monitor</h2>

<p>The first step in repairing small monitor scratches is to clean the monitor. Begin by using a soft cloth or cotton swab to gently remove any dust or debris from the surface of the monitor. Use a small amount of rubbing alcohol on the cloth or swab to help remove any stubborn dirt or grime. Make sure to avoid using too much rubbing alcohol, as it can damage the surface of the monitor.</p>

<h2>Step Two: Apply the Cleaning Solution</h2>

<p>Once the monitor has been cleaned, it is time to apply the cleaning solution. Make sure to follow the instructions on the package of the cleaning solution. Generally, the solution should be applied with a soft cloth or cotton swab and wiped in a circular motion. Allow the solution to sit for a few minutes before wiping it off with a clean cloth or swab.</p>

<h2>Step Three: Apply the Repair Solution</h2>

<p>The next step is to apply the repair solution. Begin by using a soft cloth or cotton swab to gently apply the repair solution to the scratched areas. Again, make sure to follow the instructions on the package of the repair solution. Allow the solution to sit for a few minutes before wiping it off with a clean cloth or swab.</p>

<h2>Step Four: Clean Again</h2>

<p>The final step is to clean the monitor again. This will help to remove any excess solution and leave the monitor with a clean, polished look. Again, use a small amount of rubbing alcohol on a soft cloth or cotton swab to help remove any residual solution. Make sure to avoid using too much rubbing alcohol, as it can damage the surface of the monitor.</p>
