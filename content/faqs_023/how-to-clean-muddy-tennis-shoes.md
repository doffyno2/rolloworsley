<p>Muddy tennis shoes can be a nuisance, but with the right technique, it doesn't have to be. Cleaning muddy tennis shoes is a simple process that requires a few basic things. This article will explain the proper way to clean muddy tennis shoes and make them look as good as new.</p>

<h2>What You'll Need to Clean Muddy Tennis Shoes</h2>

<p>Before you begin cleaning your muddy tennis shoes, you'll need to gather the following materials:</p>

<ul>
    <li>A brush or cloth</li>
    <li>Dish soap</li>
    <li>A sponge</li>
    <li>Clean water</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Clean Muddy Tennis Shoes</h2>

<p>Once you have gathered the materials you need, it's time to get started. Here are the steps you should follow:</p>

<ol>
    <li>Start by brushing off any excess mud from the shoes. Use a brush or cloth to gently remove the mud. This will help get rid of any large chunks of mud that could be difficult to remove later.</li>
    <li>Mix a small amount of dish soap with clean water. Dip a sponge in the mixture and use it to lightly scrub the shoes. Make sure to get into all the nooks and crannies of the shoes to get rid of any stubborn dirt.</li>
    <li>Once you've scrubbed the shoes, rinse them off with clean water. Use a cloth to remove any excess soap residue.</li>
    <li>Let the shoes air dry. Do not put them in the dryer or use any kind of heat to speed up the drying process.</li>
</ol>
