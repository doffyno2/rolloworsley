<h2>What Is a Maintenance Light?</h2>

<p>A maintenance light is a warning light on your dashboard that informs you that the vehicle needs to be serviced or repaired. The light is typically yellow or orange and will appear when the vehicle needs attention. It could be an oil change, a tire rotation, or a more serious issue. When the light appears, it is important to take your car in for service as soon as possible.</p>

<h2>Why Is the Maintenance Light On?</h2>

<p>The maintenance light can come on for a variety of reasons. Most of the time, it is a simple issue such as an oil change or tire rotation that needs to be taken care of. If the light does not go away after you have serviced your car, it is important to have it checked out by a professional mechanic.</p>

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light on a 2013 Honda Pilot</h2>

<p>Resetting the maintenance light on a 2013 Honda Pilot is a relatively simple process. The first step is to turn the ignition key to the "On" position, and then press the "Select/Reset" button on the dashboard. The maintenance light should start flashing. Next, press the select/reset button again, and the light should go off. If it does not go off, you may need to repeat the process. It is important to note that this process may vary depending on the type of Honda Pilot you have.</p>
