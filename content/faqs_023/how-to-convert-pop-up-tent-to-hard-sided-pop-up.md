How to Convert a Pop Up Tent to a Hard Sided Pop Up


Many people enjoy camping in pop up tents. However, pop up tents can be difficult to set up and take down, and they can be flimsy and prone to leaking. Hard sided pop up tents are a great alternative for those who don’t mind spending a bit more money for a more durable, heavier tent. Converting a pop up tent to a hard sided pop up is not difficult and can be done with a few simple supplies. 

<h2>Materials Needed for the Conversion</h2>

To convert a pop up tent to a hard sided pop up, you will need the following materials:

<ul>
<li>Plywood</li>
<li>Screws</li>
<li>Drill</li>
<li>Screwdriver</li>
<li>Measuring tape</li>
<li>Circular saw (optional)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step-by-Step Guide to Converting a Pop Up Tent to a Hard Sided Pop Up</h2>

Once you have gathered the necessary materials, you can begin the conversion process. 

<h3>Step 1: Measure the Tent</h3>

The first step is to measure the tent. You will need to measure the length, width, and height of the tent to determine the size of the plywood pieces you will need to build the frame. 

<h3>Step 2: Cut the Plywood to Size</h3>

Once you have the measurements, you will need to cut the plywood to size. If you do not have access to a circular saw, you can have the plywood cut to size at your local hardware store. 

<h3>Step 3: Assemble the Frame</h3>

Once you have the plywood cut to size, it’s time to assemble the frame. You will need to use the screws and drill to secure the pieces of plywood together. Make sure the frame is sturdy and secure before proceeding. 

<h3>Step 4: Attach the Frame to the Tent</h3>

Once the frame is assembled, you will need to attach it to the tent. You will need to use the screws and drill to secure the frame to the tent. Make sure the frame is securely attached before proceeding. 

<h3>Step 5: Secure the Frame</h3>

Once the frame is attached to the tent, you will need to secure the frame with more screws. Make sure the frame is securely attached before proceeding. 

<h3>Step 6: Enjoy Your Hard Sided Pop Up Tent</h3>

Once the frame is secure, you can enjoy your hard sided pop up tent. You can now enjoy camping in a more sturdy, durable tent that is easier to set up and take down.
