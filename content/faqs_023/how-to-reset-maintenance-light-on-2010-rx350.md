<h2>What Does the Maintenance Light Mean?</h2>

The maintenance light on a 2010 Lexus RX 350 is an indicator light that is designed to alert the driver to any upcoming maintenance needs. It is important to know what the light means and how to reset it. This light is usually orange or yellow and looks like an exclamation mark in a circle. It is located on the instrument panel and will turn on when the vehicle needs to be serviced.

<h2>Why You Should Reset the Maintenance Light</h2>

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light</h2>

Resetting the maintenance light on a 2010 Lexus RX 350 is a relatively simple process. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to reset the maintenance light on a 2010 Lexus RX 350:

<h3>Step 1: Locate the OBD-II Port</h3>

The first step to resetting the maintenance light is to locate the OBD-II port. This port is usually located under the steering wheel of the vehicle. It is a small, square-shaped hole that is usually black in color.

<h3>Step 2: Plug in the OBD-II Scanner</h3>

Once you have located the OBD-II port, it is time to plug in the OBD-II scanner. This scanner is a device that can be purchased at most auto parts stores. It is designed to read codes and reset the maintenance light.

<h3>Step 3: Scan for Codes</h3>

Once the OBD-II scanner is plugged in, it is time to scan for codes. The scanner will read any trouble codes that may be present. It is important to note any codes that are displayed. Make sure to take note of the codes so that you can have them checked when you take your vehicle to the service center.

<h3>Step 4: Reset the Maintenance Light</h3>

Once any codes have been noted, it is time to reset the maintenance light. Most OBD-II scanners have a dedicated button or menu item for resetting the maintenance light. Simply press the button or select the menu item and the maintenance light will be reset.
