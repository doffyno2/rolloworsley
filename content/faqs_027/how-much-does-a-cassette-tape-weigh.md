<h2>What is a Cassette Tape?</h2>

A cassette tape is a type of analog audio recording that uses magnetic tape to capture sound. It was first used in the 1950s in Europe and Asia, and became popular in the United States in the 1970s as a cheaper alternative to vinyl records. The cassette tape consists of two reels of tape, each containing a single track. The tape is wound around the reels and passes through a mechanism that reads and writes the audio signal.
