<h2>What Are Transmission Bushings?</h2>

<p>Transmission bushings are rubber or polyurethane components that are used to cushion the transmission from the engine. They are designed to absorb the vibration and shock that occurs when the engine is running and the transmission is shifting. They also act as a lubricant, reducing friction and wear on the transmission components. </p>

<h2>What Causes Transmission Bushings To Wear Out?</h2>

<p>The most common cause of transmission bushing wear is age. As the vehicle gets older, the rubber or polyurethane material that makes up the bushing begins to degrade, resulting in a loss of cushioning and lubrication. In some cases, a transmission bushing may become damaged due to an impact or other outside force. </p>

<h2>What Are The Signs Of A Worn Out Transmission Bushing?</h2>

<p>The most common sign of a worn out transmission bushing is a clunking sound when the vehicle shifts gears. This is caused by the lack of cushioning between the engine and transmission, resulting in a direct shock when the gears are shifted. Other signs include difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, and increased vibration when the vehicle is in motion. </p>
