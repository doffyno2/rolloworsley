<p>Frozen fish is a great way to enjoy fresh seafood without having to worry about the hassle of going to the store and buying fresh fish. But if you're planning to cook it, you'll need to know how long to defrost frozen fish in water. This article will help you understand the process and give you tips on how to defrost your fish quickly and safely. </p>

<h2>What is the Best Way to Defrost Frozen Fish?</h2>

<p>The best way to defrost frozen fish is in cold water. You can use a sink or a bowl filled with cold water. Add the frozen fish to the water and let it sit for about 30 minutes. It should be thawed enough to break apart easily, but still cold enough to keep it from cooking. You can also submerge the frozen fish in a zip-top bag to avoid having to deal with any water splashing. </p>

<h2>How Long Does it Take to Defrost Frozen Fish?</h2>

<p>It usually takes about 30 minutes for a small piece of frozen fish to defrost. Larger pieces of frozen fish, such as whole fish, will take longer to defrost. Generally, it takes about an hour for a medium-sized piece of fish to defrost. For whole fish, it can take up to two hours or more. </p>

<h2>Tips for Defrosting Frozen Fish Quickly and Safely</h2>

<p>When defrosting frozen fish, it's important to do it quickly and safely. Here are some tips to help you do just that:</p>

<ul>
<li>Do not leave the fish in the water for more than 30 minutes. This can cause the fish to start cooking, which can make it unsafe to eat.</li>
<li>Do not defrost frozen fish in the microwave. This can cause the fish to become tough and dry.</li>
<li>Make sure to change the water every 15 minutes, as this will help the defrosting process go more quickly.</li>
<li>If you are not able to change the water every 15 minutes, you can add a few ice cubes to the water to help keep it cold.</li>
<li>Once the fish is thawed, you should cook it immediately. Do not refreeze it.</li>
</ul>
