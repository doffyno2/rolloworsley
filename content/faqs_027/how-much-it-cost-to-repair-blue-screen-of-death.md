<h2>What Is The Blue Screen Of Death?</h2>

<p>The Blue Screen of Death (BSoD) is a Microsoft Windows error message that is displayed when the Windows operating system encounters a critical error. It typically appears as a blue screen with white text and an error message. The exact error message can vary, but it usually indicates a problem with a computer's hardware or software. The BSoD is most commonly caused by hardware or driver issues, but can also be caused by viruses, malware, or other software-related problems.</p>

<h2>What Causes The Blue Screen Of Death?</h2>

<p>The Blue Screen of Death can be caused by a variety of issues, including hardware or driver problems, viruses, malware, or other software-related problems. Hardware problems can include failing hardware components, such as a hard drive, RAM, or CPU, or a driver issue. Driver issues can include outdated, corrupt, or incompatible drivers. Viruses and malware can cause the BSoD by corrupting system files or causing the system to malfunction.</p>

<h2>How Much Does It Cost To Repair The Blue Screen Of Death?</h2>

<p>The cost of repairing the Blue Screen of Death will vary depending on the cause of the problem. Hardware repair costs can vary widely, depending on the type of hardware that needs to be replaced. Generally, repairing a hard drive, RAM, or CPU will cost between $100 and $500, depending on the make and model of the hardware. Replacing a defective graphics card can cost up to $400, while replacing the motherboard can cost up to $700. For driver issues, the cost of repair may be lower; however, it can still range from $50 to $200.</p>
