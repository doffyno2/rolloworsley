<h2>How Long Does Chicken Soup Last Out of the Fridge?</h2>

In general, chicken soup will last for up to four hours out of the fridge. This includes the time that it takes to get the soup to its serving temperature. After four hours, the soup should be discarded as it is no longer safe to eat.

<h2>How to Store Chicken Soup</h2>

When storing chicken soup, it's important to make sure it's stored in an airtight container. If the container is not airtight, the soup can spoil quickly due to bacteria growth. Additionally, the container should be placed in the refrigerator as soon as possible after cooking. If the soup is left out at room temperature for more than two hours, it should be discarded.

<h2>Reheating Chicken Soup</h2>

If you need to reheat chicken soup, make sure to do so thoroughly. The soup should be heated to an internal temperature of 165 degrees Fahrenheit and all parts of the soup should be steaming hot. Additionally, the soup should not be reheated more than once.

<h2>Can You Freeze Leftover Chicken Soup?</h2>

Yes, you can freeze leftover chicken soup. However, it's important to make sure it's stored in an airtight container or freezer bag. Additionally, be sure to label the container with the date the soup was cooked. Frozen chicken soup will last for up to three months.
