<h2>Types of Fiber Optic Line Repairs</h2>

<p>Fiber optic lines can be damaged in a variety of ways, including physical damage, environmental damage, and human error. Depending on the type of damage, the repair process can be more or less expensive. Common types of fiber optic line repairs include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Physical damage repair: This type of repair is typically the most expensive, as it requires specialized equipment and labor to fix the line. This type of repair is most common in cases of vandalism, accidents, or extreme weather events.</li>
	<li>Environmental damage repair: Environmental damage includes things like corrosion, water damage, or insect infestations. These repairs are typically less expensive than physical damage repairs, as they require less labor and specialized equipment.</li>
	<li>Human error repair: Human error repairs are usually the least expensive, as they often require only minor repairs or adjustments. Common causes of human error damage include incorrect installation, incorrect splicing, and incorrect routing.</li>
</ul>
