<h2>What Causes Rod Knock?</h2>

<p>Rod knock is caused by worn or damaged engine bearings. These are located inside the engine and are responsible for supporting the connecting rods, which link the crankshaft to the pistons. When the bearings wear out, the connecting rods become loose, resulting in a knocking noise that is often referred to as a “rod knock.”</p>

<p>In some cases, rod knock may be caused by a lack of lubrication or by an incorrect oil viscosity. This can lead to the bearings becoming dry, which will cause them to wear out and eventually lead to rod knock.</p>

<h2>What are the Symptoms of Rod Knock?</h2>

<p>If you’re experiencing rod knock, you may notice a knocking noise coming from the engine bay when the engine is running. This noise is usually more pronounced when the engine is under load, such as when accelerating or going up a hill. In some cases, the noise may be present even at idle.</p>

<p>In addition to the knocking noise, you may also notice a decrease in performance due to the lack of power caused by the rod knock. Your vehicle may also be consuming more fuel than normal, as the engine has to work harder to compensate for the worn bearings.</p>

<h2>How Can You Diagnose Rod Knock?</h2>

<p>If you think you may be experiencing rod knock, you should take your vehicle to a mechanic or dealership.
