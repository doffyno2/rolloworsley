<h2>What is a Cylinder Lock?</h2>

A cylinder lock is a type of lock that uses a cylinder to secure the door. Cylinder locks are usually installed on doors in commercial and residential buildings. Generally, the cylinder lock is mounted on the outside of the door and the key is used to open the door from the inside.

<h2>What Tools Are Required to Break a Cylinder Lock?</h2>

In order to break a cylinder lock, you will need a few tools. These include a hammer, a flathead screwdriver, a chisel, and a pair of pliers. You may also need a pry bar if needed. 

<h2>Steps to Breaking a Cylinder Lock</h2>

<h3>Step One: Remove the Lock Casing</h3>

The first step to breaking a cylinder lock is to remove the lock casing. To do this, use a flathead screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the casing in place. Once the screws have been removed, the casing should come off with ease. 

<h3>Step Two: Insert the Chisel</h3>

Once the lock casing has been removed, you will need to insert the chisel into the keyhole. Insert the chisel into the keyhole and move it around until you feel it make contact with the cylinder inside the lock. Once the chisel is in place, use a hammer to tap it into the cylinder. 

<h3>Step Three: Pry Open the Lock</h3>

Once the chisel is in place, use a pry bar or a pair of pliers to pry open the lock. You may need to apply some force in order to pry the lock open. Once the lock is open, you should be able to remove the cylinder from the door.
