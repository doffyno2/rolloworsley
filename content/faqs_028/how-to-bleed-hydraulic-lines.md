<h2>What Are Hydraulic Lines?</h2>

Hydraulic lines are pipes or tubes that transfer pressurized fluid in order to power machinery. The pressurized fluid is usually oil or hydraulic fluid, and it is used to power the machinery. Hydraulic lines are used in a variety of machinery, from tractors and cars to cranes and construction machinery.

<h2>What Is Bleeding Hydraulic Lines?</h2>

Bleeding hydraulic lines is the process of removing air and other contaminants from the hydraulic system. This is done by opening the bleed valves in the hydraulic system and allowing the pressurized fluid to flow out. This removes any air in the system and prevents any air pockets from forming, which can cause damage to the system.
