<h2>What Is Tension?</h2>

<p>Tension is the force that is put on the chain and sprocket of a bike. The tension keeps the chain from slipping off the sprockets and ensures that the bike is running smoothly. If the tension is too low, the chain can slip off the sprockets, resulting in a loss of power or even an accident. If the tension is too high, it can put extra strain on the bike, causing it to wear out faster.</p>

<h2>How to Adjust Tension on an Echelon Bike?</h2>

<p>Adjusting the tension on an Echelon bike is relatively straightforward. The first step is to locate the tensioner. On most Echelon bikes, the tensioner will be located near the rear wheel. It is usually a small round device that is connected to the chain and sprocket. </p> 

<p>Once you have located the tensioner, you will need to adjust the tension. To do this, use a wrench to adjust the tensioner. Turn the wrench clockwise to increase the tension, and turn the wrench counter-clockwise to decrease the tension. Turn the wrench until the chain is tight, but not so tight that it is causing extra strain on the bike.</p>

<p>Once you have adjusted the tension to the desired level, you can then test it. To do this, rotate the pedals and listen for any squeaking or rattling sounds. If you hear any noises, then you may need to adjust the tension again. Continue to adjust the tension until you no longer hear any noises.</p>

<p>When adjusting the tension on your Echelon bike, it is important to keep a few things in mind. First, make sure to turn the wrench slowly and in small increments. Doing so will ensure that you do not over-tighten or under-tighten the tension. Second, always use a wrench that is the correct size for the tensioner. Using the wrong size wrench can cause damage to the tensioner or the bike itself.</p>
