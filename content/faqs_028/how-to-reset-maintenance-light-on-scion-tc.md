<p>Your Scion TC's maintenance light is an important indicator of the health of your car. When the light comes on, it's a sign that you need to take your car in for maintenance. Fortunately, resetting the light is a simple process that you can do yourself. In this article, we'll cover the steps you need to take to reset the maintenance light on your Scion TC.</p>

<h2>Why Does the Maintenance Light Come On?</h2>

<p>The maintenance light on your Scion TC is designed to remind you to take your car in for regular maintenance. This includes oil changes, tire rotations, fluid flushes, and more. By keeping up with these maintenance tasks, you can ensure that your car is running optimally and safely. </p>

<h2>What Does the Maintenance Light Look Like?</h2>

<p>The maintenance light on your Scion TC will be a yellow or orange light with a wrench icon. If you see this light on your dashboard, it's time to take your car in for service.</p>

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light on a Scion TC</h2>

<p>The process for resetting the maintenance light on your Scion TC is relatively simple. It requires a few steps, but can be done in just a few minutes. Here's what you need to do:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Turn Off the Ignition</h3>

<p>Turn off the ignition on your Scion TC. This will turn off all of the car's electrical systems.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Disconnect the Battery</h3>

<p>Next, you'll need to disconnect the battery from the car. This can be done by disconnecting the negative battery cable. Make sure to leave the cable disconnected for at least one minute.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Reconnect the Battery</h3>

<p>After one minute has passed, reconnect the battery to the car. This will reset the car's electrical systems.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Turn On the Ignition</h3>

<p>Once the battery is reconnected, turn on the ignition and start the car. The maintenance light should now be reset.</p>
