It is not uncommon for dog owners to wonder how to make their dog's ears lay down. Dogs are unique animals and each one has its own individual personality and behavior traits. Some dogs have ears that are naturally floppy or stand upright, while others have ears that are more stubborn and refuse to lay flat. If your pup has ears that just won’t stay down, don’t worry! There are a few things you can do to help your pup achieve a more desirable look.

<h2>Understanding Dog Ears</h2>

Before you attempt to make your dog’s ears lay down, it’s important to understand why they may not want to cooperate. Dogs come in different shapes and sizes and each breed has a different type of ears. Some breeds, such as the Australian Shepherd, have upright ears, while others, such as the Cocker Spaniel, have floppy ears. Some breeds, such as the Bulldog, have ears that stand up and then flop over.

In addition to breed type, some dogs may have ears that are naturally more stubborn than others. This can be due to genetics, diet, or even environment. No matter what the cause, it’s important to remember that there are some things you can do to help your pup achieve the look you desire.

<h2>Tips For Helping Your Dog's Ears Lay Down</h2>

<h3>Regular Grooming</h3>

Regular grooming is essential to helping your pup’s ears lay down. Brush your pup’s ears at least once a week to remove any dirt, oil, or wax. This will help keep the ears clean and make them more pliable. If you notice any waxy buildup or redness, take your pup to the vet for further assessment.

<h3>Ear Cleaners</h3>

There are a variety of ear cleaners available that can help keep your pup’s ears healthy and free of debris. Be sure to choose one made specifically for dogs, as some products may irritate the sensitive area of the ear. Follow the instructions on the package for best results.

<h3>Ear Plugs</h3>

Ear plugs help to keep the ear closed, which can help encourage the ear to lay down. These are best used in conjunction with regular grooming, as it is important to keep the ear clean and free of any debris.

<h3>Ear Massage</h3>

Massaging the area around your pup’s ears can help encourage the ears to lay down. Use your fingertips to gently rub the area in a circular motion. This can be done a few times a day and will help to naturally relax the muscles in the area and encourage the ears to lay down.

<h3>Taping</h3>

Taping the ears can help to gently encourage the ears to lay down. You can use medical tape or purchase a product specifically designed for taping ears. Be sure to follow the instructions and avoid taping too tightly, as this can cause irritation.

<h3>Exercise</h3>

Regular exercise can help to strengthen the muscles in the area, which should in turn, help the ears to lay down. Take your pup for regular walks or play fetch in the yard to help keep him active.
