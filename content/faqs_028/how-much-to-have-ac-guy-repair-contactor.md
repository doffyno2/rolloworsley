<h2>What is a Contactor in an Air Conditioner?</h2>

<p>A contactor, or contact switch, is a device used in air conditioning systems to control the flow of electricity to the compressor. This device is responsible for turning the compressor off and on in order to maintain the desired temperature inside the home. The contactor is typically located near the outdoor unit and is connected to the compressor by two wires. If the contactor malfunctions, it can prevent the compressor from operating correctly, causing the air conditioner to become inefficient or even not work at all.</p>
