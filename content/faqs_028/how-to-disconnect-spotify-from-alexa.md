<p>If you’ve been using Alexa to control your Spotify account, you may have recently decided that you want to disconnect the two services. Whether you’re switching to a different music streaming service or just want to free up some space on your device, disconnecting Alexa and Spotify can be a simple process.</p> 

<p>In this article, we’ll discuss the steps you’ll need to take to disconnect Spotify from Alexa and how to set up a new music streaming service. We’ll also answer some frequently asked questions about Alexa and Spotify, so let’s get started.</p>

<h2>Steps to Disconnect Spotify from Alexa</h2>

<p>If you want to disconnect Spotify from Alexa, here’s what you’ll need to do:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Open the Alexa App</h3>

<p>The first step is to open the Alexa app on your mobile device or computer. Once the app is open, go to the menu and select “Settings”.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Select Music & Podcasts</h3>

<p>Scroll down to the “Music & Podcasts” section and select “Link New Service”. This will take you to a page where you can link or unlink various music streaming services.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Unlink Spotify</h3>

<p>Scroll down to the “Spotify” section and select “Unlink”. This will disconnect Spotify from your Alexa device.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Link a New Service</h3>

<p>If you’re looking to link a new music streaming service, you can do so by selecting the “Link New Service” option. This will take you to a page where you can select the service you’d like to link.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Is it easy to disconnect Spotify from Alexa?</h3>

<p>Yes, it is very easy to disconnect Spotify from Alexa. All you need to do is open the Alexa app and select the “Unlink” option for Spotify.</p>

<h3>Can I link a different music streaming service to Alexa?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can link a different music streaming service to Alexa. All you need to do is open the Alexa app and select the “Link New Service” option.</p>

<h3>What happens when I disconnect Spotify from Alexa?</h3>

<p>When you disconnect Spotify from Alexa, you will no longer be able to control your Spotify account with your Alexa device. However, you can still access your Spotify account through the Spotify app.</p>

<h3>Can I still use Spotify with Alexa?</h3>

<p>No, once you have disconnected Spotify from Alexa, you will no longer be able to control your Spotify account with your Alexa device. You will need to link a different music streaming service to your Alexa device.</p>

<h3>Do I need to pay for a different music streaming service?</h3>

<p>Yes, depending on the service you choose, you may need to pay for a subscription in order to access the content. However, many services offer free trials or discounted rates.</p>

<h3>Can I still use Alexa to control my other devices?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can still use Alexa to control other devices, such as your lights, thermostat, and more. Disconnecting Spotify from Alexa will only affect your ability to control your Spotify account.</p>

<h3>Can I reconnect Spotify to Alexa?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can reconnect Spotify to Alexa at any time. All you need to do is open the Alexa app and select the “Link” option for Spotify.</p>

<h3>Can I link multiple music streaming services to Alexa?</h3>

<p>Yes, you can link multiple music streaming services to Alexa. All you need to do is open the Alexa app and select the “Link New Service” option.</p>

<h3>What happens if I disconnect Spotify from Alexa and then reconnect it?</h3>

<p>If you disconnect Spotify from Alexa and then reconnect it, all of your previous settings and preferences will be restored. You will be able to control your Spotify account with your Alexa device as you did before disconnecting.</p>
