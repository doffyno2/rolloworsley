<p>Having a broken window crank is a common problem for many Andersen window owners. Fortunately, it is possible to repair the crank and bring your window back to good working order without the need for a professional. Read on to find out how to repair your broken Andersen window crank.</p>

<h2>What You Need</h2>

<p>Before you start, make sure you have the following items:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Screwdriver</li>
    <li>Replacement crank</li>
    <li>Lubricant</li>
</ul>

<h2>Instructions</h2>

<p>Follow these steps to repair your Andersen window crank:</p>

<ol>
    <li>Remove the window crank. Use a screwdriver to unscrew the screws that are holding the crank in place. You may need to use a bit of force to remove the crank.</li>
    <li>Inspect the crank. Take a close look at the crank and make sure that it is not broken or damaged in any way. If the crank is damaged, you will need to replace it with a new one.</li>
    <li>Clean the crank. Use a cloth and some lubricant to clean the crank. This will help to make sure that it works properly when you re-install it.</li>
    <li>Re-install the crank. Put the crank back into place and use the screwdriver to screw it back into place. Make sure that the screws are securely tightened.</li>
    <li>Test the crank. Once the crank is re-installed, open and close the window a few times to make sure that it is working properly.</li>
</ol>

<h3>What type of screwdriver do I need to remove the window crank?</h3>

<p>The type of screwdriver you will need will depend on the type of screws that are holding the crank in place. Most cranks use standard Phillips head screws, so a Phillips head screwdriver should work.</p>

<p>If the window crank is still not working properly after you have re-installed it, you may need to replace it with a new one.</p>
