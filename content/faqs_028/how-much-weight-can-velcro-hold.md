<h2>What is Velcro?</h2>

Velcro is a fastening system that was invented in the 1940s by Swiss engineer George de Mestral. It consists of two components: two strips of fabric material with a series of small hooks on one side and a series of small loops on the other. When the two strips are pressed together, the hooks catch onto the loops, providing a secure, yet adjustable connection.

<h2>How Much Weight Can Velcro Hold?</h2>

The amount of weight that Velcro can hold depends on several factors, including the type of Velcro used, the quality of the adhesive, and the surface the Velcro is attached to.
