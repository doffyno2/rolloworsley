<h2>What Causes a Soft Spot in Floor?</h2>

<p>Soft spots in flooring can be caused by a variety of things, including water damage, warping, and more. Water damage is one of the most common causes of soft spots in flooring, as water can seep into the floorboards and create a soft spot. Warping can also cause a soft spot, as the wood can warp due to changes in temperature and humidity. In some cases, a soft spot in the floor can be caused by damage to the subflooring, such as dry rot.</p>

<p>It's important to identify the cause of the soft spot in your floor before attempting to repair it, as it will determine the best course of action. If the soft spot is caused by water damage, it's important to address the source of the water issue before attempting to repair the spot.</p>
