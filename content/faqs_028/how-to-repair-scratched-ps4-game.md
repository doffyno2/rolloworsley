<p>If you own a PlayStation 4, you know the frustration of trying to play a scratched game. Scratches on the disc can make it difficult to play, and in some cases, it's impossible. Fortunately, there are a few ways to fix a scratched PS4 game. Read on to learn how you can repair a scratched PS4 game and get back to playing.</p>

<h2>What Causes Disc Scratches on PS4 Games?</h2>

<p>Disc scratches can be caused by a variety of factors. The most common cause of disc scratches is simply wear and tear. Over time, the disc may become scratched due to handling or storage. Another common cause of disc scratches is dirt or debris on the disc. If the disc is not cleaned properly, dirt and debris can accumulate and cause scratches.</p>

<h2>How to Repair a Scratched PS4 Game</h2>

<h3>Clean the Disc</h3>

<p>The first step in repairing a scratched PS4 game is to clean the disc. Start by using a lint-free cloth to wipe away any dirt or debris. If the disc is still scratched, you can try using a disc repair kit. These kits usually come with a cleaning solution and a brush or polishing pad. Apply the cleaning solution to the disc and gently scrub away any scratches.</p>

<h3>Use a Disc Repair Machine</h3>

<p>If cleaning the disc doesn't work, you can try using a disc repair machine. These machines use a combination of heat and pressure to fill in the scratches on the disc. They are relatively inexpensive and can be found in most electronics stores. Simply follow the instructions on the machine to repair the disc.</p>

<h3>Replace the Disc</h3>

<p>If the disc is still scratched after cleaning and using a disc repair machine, it's time to replace the disc. Many stores will allow you to exchange a scratched disc for a new one. You may also be able to purchase a new copy of the game online. Keep in mind, however, that you may need to purchase an additional license for the game.</p>

<h3>Prevent Further Damage</h3>

<p>Once you've repaired or replaced the disc, it's important to take steps to prevent further damage. Make sure to store the disc in a protective case when not in use. Also, be sure to keep the disc clean by wiping it off with a lint-free cloth before and after each use.</p>
