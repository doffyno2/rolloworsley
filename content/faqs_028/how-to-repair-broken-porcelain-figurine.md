Porcelain figurines are delicate and intricate works of art. They can be passed down through generations, or treasured as memories of a special time or place. Unfortunately, they can also break, leaving the owner with a treasured item that is in need of repair. Fortunately, it is possible to repair a broken porcelain figurine. With the right tools and a bit of patience, it is possible to restore a broken porcelain figurine back to its former beauty.

<h2>What You Need</h2>

The first step to repairing a broken porcelain figurine is to gather the right supplies. You will need:

<ul>
	<li>A clean, dry surface</li>
	<li>Porcelain glue</li>
	<li>A soft cloth</li>
	<li>Fine-grain sandpaper</li>
	<li>A toothpick</li>
	<li>A cotton swab</li>
	<li>A small paintbrush</li>
	<li>A container of water</li>
	<li>A clean, dry towel</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps to Repair a Broken Porcelain Figurine</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Clean the Broken Pieces</h3>

The first step is to clean the broken pieces. Use a damp cloth to wipe away any dirt or debris. Make sure the pieces are completely dry before proceeding.

<h3>Step 2: Apply Porcelain Glue</h3>

Once the pieces are dry, itâ€™s time to apply the porcelain glue. Use the toothpick to apply a thin layer of the glue to the edges of the broken pieces. Be sure to cover the entire area.

<h3>Step 3: Place the Pieces Together</h3>

Once the glue has been applied, place the broken pieces together. Gently press the pieces together until they are firmly in place.

<h3>Step 4: Dry and Sand the Figurine</h3>

Allow the figurine to dry for at least 24 hours. Once it is dry, use the fine-grain sandpaper to gently sand away any rough edges. You can also use the cotton swab and water to remove any remaining glue.

<h3>Step 5: Paint the Figurine</h3>

Finally, you can paint the figurine to match the original color. Use a small paintbrush and the paint color of your choice. Allow the paint to dry before displaying the figurine.
