<p>Clay sewer pipes are a popular choice for plumbing systems due to their durability and low cost. However, clay sewer pipes can break due to age, heavy use, or large tree roots invading the pipe. When a clay sewer pipe does break, the pipe must be repaired in order to prevent further damage and costly repairs in the future. In this article, we’ll discuss how to repair a broken clay sewer pipe.</p>

<h2>What Causes Clay Sewer Pipes To Break?</h2>

<p>Clay sewer pipes are susceptible to breaks due to age, heavy use, and large tree roots. Over time, clay sewer pipes can corrode and weaken, leading to cracks and breaks. Heavy use can cause the pipe to sag, resulting in breaks and leaks. Large tree roots can invade the pipe and cause breaks, as well. It’s important to inspect your clay sewer pipe regularly to ensure it’s not showing signs of wear or damage.</p>

<h2>How To Repair A Broken Clay Sewer Pipe</h2>

<p>Repairing a broken clay sewer pipe is a job that should be left to a professional. Attempting to repair a broken clay pipe without professional help can lead to further damage and costly repairs. A professional plumber will assess the condition of the pipe, determine the best course of action, and complete the repair. Here’s how a professional plumber will repair a broken clay sewer pipe:</p>

<h3>Assessing The Pipe</h3>

<p>The first step in repairing a broken clay sewer pipe is to assess the condition of the pipe. The plumber will inspect the pipe to determine the cause of the break and the best course of action. Depending on the severity of the break, the plumber may recommend a repair or a replacement.</p>

<h3>Repairing The Pipe</h3>

<p>If the plumber determines that a repair is possible, they will begin the repair process. The plumber will clean the pipe and prepare it for repair. They will then use a special sealing material to fill the crack or hole in the pipe. Once the repair is complete, the plumber will test the pipe to ensure it’s functioning properly.</p>

<h3>Replacing The Pipe</h3>

<p>If the plumber determines that the pipe is too damaged to repair, they will recommend replacing the pipe. The plumber will remove the old pipe and replace it with a new one. The new pipe will be tested to make sure there are no leaks or other issues. Once the pipe is installed, the plumber will clean up the area and make sure the new pipe is functioning correctly.</p>
