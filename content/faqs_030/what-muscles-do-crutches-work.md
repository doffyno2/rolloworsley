Crutches are a type of mobility device that provides stability and support for individuals with walking or balance issues. Crutches are used to assist with walking, standing, or sitting. They are designed to provide stability and reduce the risk of falls. Crutches are typically used for short-term mobility until the individual can walk unassisted.

<h2>What Are the Benefits of Using Crutches?</h2>

Crutches provide a number of benefits to those who require assistance with mobility. They are a safe and effective way to reduce the risk of falls and to provide stability while walking. Crutches also help to reduce the amount of pressure on the lower body, which can help to reduce pain and swelling. Additionally, crutches can help to improve balance and stability while walking.

<h2>What Muscles Do Crutches Work?</h2>

Crutches work a number of muscles in the body. The primary muscles that are used when using crutches are the triceps, deltoids, and biceps. The triceps are responsible for extending the elbow and helping to stabilize the arms while walking. The deltoids are responsible for lifting the arms and helping to maintain balance while walking. The biceps are responsible for flexing the elbow and helping to control the arms while walking.

<h2>How to Use Crutches Properly</h2>

In order to use crutches safely and effectively, there are a few steps that need to be taken. First, the crutches should be adjusted to the correct height for the individual. The handles should be at waist height and the arm rests should be at elbow height. This will ensure that the individual is using the crutches properly.

Once the crutches are adjusted properly, the individual should practice walking with them. This is best done indoors on a flat surface. The individual should practice walking with one crutch at a time, as this will help to ensure that the muscles used to support the body are strong enough to support the individual's weight.
