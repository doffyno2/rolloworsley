<h2>What is Frequency?</h2>

Frequency is a measure of how often a wave or vibration occurs over a given period of time. It is measured in hertz (Hz) and is usually expressed as a range. For example, 20Hz to 20,000Hz is considered the standard range for human hearing. Low frequencies are those that are lower than 20Hz and higher frequencies are those that are higher than 20,000Hz.

<h2>How Does Frequency Affect Sound?</h2>

The frequency of a sound wave determines its pitch and volume. Low frequency sounds have a deeper, lower pitch while high frequency sounds have a higher, sharper pitch. Low frequency sounds are also less intense than high frequency sounds. This means that low frequency sound waves can travel further than high frequency sound waves because they don't dissipate as quickly.

<h2>How Does Low Frequency Sound Travel?</h2>

Low frequency sound waves travel in a different way than high frequency sound waves. Low frequency sound waves are slower and have longer wavelengths. This means that they travel further than high frequency sound waves before they dissipate. In addition, low frequency sound waves can pass through objects and travel through walls, floors, and ceilings more easily than high frequency sound waves.

<h2>What Are the Advantages of Low Frequency Sound?</h2>

Low frequency sound has a number of advantages. As mentioned earlier, it can travel further than high frequency sound, which makes it ideal for long-distance communication. Low frequency sound can also be used to detect objects and measure distances. Additionally, low frequency sound can penetrate through solid objects and is less likely to be blocked.

<h2>What Are the Disadvantages of Low Frequency Sound?</h2>
