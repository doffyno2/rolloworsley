<p>The timber line is the altitude at which trees are no longer able to survive due to low temperatures and lack of moisture. While the exact location of the timber line varies according to climate, it typically occurs around the 4,000-meter (13,123-feet) mark. Above this altitude, tree growth is limited, and the terrain is usually barren and rocky. In some cases, trees may grow at higher altitudes, but they are usually stunted, low-lying shrubs. </p>

<h2>What is the Difference Between the Timber Line and the Tree Line?</h2>

<p>The timber line and the tree line are often used interchangeably, but they are not the same. The tree line is the boundary between areas where trees are able to grow and those where they cannot due to environmental conditions. It is a more general term and does not refer to a specific altitude. The timber line, on the other hand, is the specific altitude at which tree growth is limited due to environmental conditions. </p>
