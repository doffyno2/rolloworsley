How to True a Motorcycle Wheel 


When you ride a motorcycle, it is important to make sure that the wheels are true and round. This ensures that the bike rides smoothly, and that the tires wear evenly. Truing a motorcycle wheel is a relatively simple process, and can be done with basic tools and supplies. In this article, we will explain how to true a motorcycle wheel, and what supplies you will need to do it.

<h2>What is Wheel Truing?</h2>

Wheel truing is the process of making sure that the wheel is round and true. This helps the bike ride more smoothly and evenly, and helps the tires last longer. A wheel that is out of true will cause the tire to wear unevenly, and may even cause the tire to fail prematurely.

<h2>Tools and Supplies You Will Need</h2>

To true a motorcycle wheel, you will need the following tools and supplies:

<ul>
<li>A truing stand</li>
<li>A truing gauge</li>
<li>A spoke wrench</li>
<li>A flat file</li>
<li>A spoke key</li>
<li>A rim strip</li>
<li>A caliper</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps to True a Motorcycle Wheel</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Mount the Wheel on the Truing Stand</h3>

The first step is to mount the wheel on the truing stand. Make sure that the wheel is securely mounted, and that the axle is clamped firmly in the stand.

<h3>Step 2: Check the Wheel for Bent Spokes</h3>

Once the wheel is mounted, use the spoke wrench to check for bent or broken spokes. If you find any bent spokes, use the spoke key to straighten them.

<h3>Step 3: Adjust the Spoke Tension</h3>

The next step is to adjust the spoke tension. This is done by turning the spoke nipples with the spoke wrench until they are all properly tensioned.

<h3>Step 4: Measure the Wheel for Trueness</h3>

Once the spoke tension is adjusted, use the truing gauge to measure the wheel for trueness. If the wheel is out of true, use the spoke wrench to adjust the spokes until the wheel is true.

<h3>Step 5: Measure the Wheel for Roundness</h3>

Once the wheel is true, use the caliper to measure the wheel for roundness. If the wheel is not round, use a flat file to file down any high spots until the wheel is round.

<h3>Step 6: Install the Rim Strip</h3>

Once the wheel is true and round, install the rim strip. This will help protect the rim and tire from damage.
