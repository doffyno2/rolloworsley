Physical barriers are a type of barrier that can impede the delivery of health and social care. They are physical elements of the environment, such as buildings, roads, or other obstacles that can prevent access to services or make it difficult for people to access them. Physical barriers can limit the effectiveness of health and social care services, and can negatively impact the quality of care that people receive.

Physical barriers can include a wide range of elements, from inadequate road infrastructure to lack of access to transportation, from inadequate facilities to inadequate staffing, from lack of resources to lack of access to technology. These can all limit the ability of health and social care organizations to deliver the best care possible to those they serve.
