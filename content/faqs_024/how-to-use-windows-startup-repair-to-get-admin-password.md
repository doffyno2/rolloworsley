<h2>What is Windows Startup Repair?</h2>

<p>Windows Startup Repair is a built-in Windows system tool that can help you troubleshoot and fix startup problems. It can also help you reset your user accounts, including the administrator account. When you use Windows Startup Repair, it will scan your computer and detect any issues that could be causing your computer to not start correctly. If it finds any issues, it will attempt to repair them automatically, which may also include resetting your user account passwords.</p>

<p>If you have forgotten your administrator password, you can use Windows Startup Repair to reset it. Here are the steps to do so:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Access the Advanced Startup Options</h3>

<p>The first step is to access the Advanced Startup Options. To do this, you need to restart your computer and press the F8 key when the logo appears. This will bring up a menu of options that you can choose from. From there, select the option to “Repair your computer”.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Select Windows Startup Repair</h3>

<p>Once you have chosen the “Repair your computer” option, you will be presented with a list of recovery tools. From here, you need to select the “Startup Repair” option. This will start the Startup Repair process, which will scan your computer and attempt to identify and fix any issues that could be preventing it from starting correctly.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Reset Your User Account Password</h3>

<p>Once the Startup Repair process is finished, it will present you with a list of user accounts that it has detected on your computer. From here, you can select the user account whose password you want to reset. Then, simply click the “Reset Password” button and follow the on-screen instructions to reset the password.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Restart Your Computer</h3>

<p>Once you have reset the user account password, you can close the Startup Repair window and restart your computer. When your computer starts, you can log in to your user account with the new password that you have just set.</p>
