<h2>Step 1: Prepare the Exhaust Pipe</h2>

<p>The first step in welding an exhaust pipe is to prepare the pipe. This includes grinding off any rust or corrosion, as well as cleaning the surface of the pipe with a wire brush. This will ensure a clean surface for the welds and will ensure that they are strong and long-lasting. </p>

<h2>Step 2: Set Up the Welding Equipment</h2>

<p>Once you have prepared the exhaust pipe, the next step is to set up the welding equipment. This includes setting up the welding machine, connecting the welding rods, and adjusting the settings for the type of weld you are doing. It is important to make sure that all the settings are correct for the type of weld you are doing, as this will ensure the welds are strong and long-lasting.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Begin Welding</h2>

<p>Once you have prepared the exhaust pipe and set up the welding equipment, you are ready to begin welding. Begin by placing the welding rod in the joint of the exhaust pipe and striking an arc. Hold the rod in place for a few seconds and then move it along the length of the joint, welding as you go. Continue welding until you have finished the entire joint. </p>

<h2>Step 4: Clean Up</h2>

<p>Once you have finished welding the exhaust pipe, it's time to clean up. Use a wire brush to remove any slag or debris from the welds. This will ensure that the welds are clean and free from any contaminants. </p>
