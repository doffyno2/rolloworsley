Make ready maintenance requests are a common practice in the rental industry. It is a request from a landlord or property manager to have a tenant's current unit cleaned and/or repaired before they move out. This request is typically made to prepare the unit for the next tenant. Make ready maintenance requests can vary from a simple scrubbing of the floors to a full renovation of the unit. 

<h2>How Does Make Ready Maintenance Work?</h2>

When a tenant moves out of a rental unit, the landlord or property manager will inspect the unit and identify any needed repairs or cleaning. The landlord will then make a make ready maintenance request for the current tenant to complete before they move out. This request can include anything from cleaning the carpets, painting the walls, or repairing broken fixtures. Once the request is approved, the tenant will be given a timeline to complete the requested repairs or cleaning. 

<h2>Why Is Make Ready Maintenance Important?</h2>

Make ready maintenance is an important part of the rental process. It ensures that the unit is in good condition for the next tenant. It also helps landlords maintain the value of their rental property and reduce the cost of repairs in the future. By completing make ready maintenance requests, tenants can help ensure that the rental unit is in good condition for the next tenant and help protect their security deposit. 

<h2>What Are the Benefits of Making Ready Maintenance?</h2>

There are several benefits of making ready maintenance. It can help landlords maintain their rental property and reduce the cost of repairs in the future. It also helps tenants protect their security deposit and ensure that the rental unit is in good condition for the next tenant. Lastly, it helps landlords and tenants maintain a good relationship by ensuring that the rental unit is in good condition before the tenant moves out.

<h2>What Are the Risks of Not Completing Make Ready Maintenance?</h2>

If make ready maintenance is not completed, it can lead to several risks for both the landlord and tenant. For landlords, not completing make ready maintenance can lead to costly repairs or cleaning in the future. It can also lead to tenant disputes if the tenant believes that the landlord did not properly inspect the unit before they moved out. For tenants, not completing make ready maintenance can lead to lost security deposits or delays in getting their security deposits back. 

<h2>How Can I Make Sure My Make Ready Maintenance Request Is Completed On Time?</h2>

The best way to ensure that make ready maintenance is completed on time is to create a timeline and stick to it. Landlords should provide tenants with a timeline for completing the requested repairs or cleaning and make sure that the tenant understands what needs to be done and by when. Tenants should keep track of their progress and make sure that they are meeting the timeline.
