<p>Milk Bone dog treats are a popular snack for many dogs, but when stored improperly, they can be susceptible to insect infestations. <h2>What Causes Bugs to Grow in Milk Bone Dog Treats?</h2>

<p>The most common cause of insect infestation in milk bone dog treats is improper storage. While milk bones are designed to be shelf-stable, they still need to be stored in a cool, dry place to prevent the growth of bacteria and mold. If stored in an area that is too humid or has access to moisture, the treats can become a breeding ground for insects. Insects are attracted to the sugar and fat in the treats, which makes them an ideal food source.</p>

<p>Another cause of insect infestation in milk bone dog treats is cross-contamination. If the treats have been exposed to insects such as flies or mites, they can bring the bugs into the packaging. It is important to keep the treats away from any potential sources of insect contamination.</p>

<h2>How to Prevent Insect Infestation in Milk Bone Dog Treats</h2>

<p>The best way to prevent insect infestation in milk bone dog treats is to store them in a cool, dry place. If possible, keep the treats in an airtight container to prevent cross-contamination. Additionally, it is important to check the expiration date on the package and discard any expired treats to prevent the growth of bacteria and mold.</p>

<p>If you suspect that your milk bone dog treats have been infested with insects, it is important to discard the treats and thoroughly clean the area where they were stored. This will help to prevent the spread of the insects and protect your pup from a nasty surprise.</p>
