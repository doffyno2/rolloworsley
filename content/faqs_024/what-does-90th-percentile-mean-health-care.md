# What Does 90th Percentile Mean in Health Care?

Understanding the meaning of the 90th percentile can be a valuable tool for healthcare providers, especially in the context of quality of care. This article explains what the 90th percentile means in healthcare, how it is used, and what it can tell healthcare providers about their care.

## What Is the 90th Percentile?

The 90th percentile is a measure of quality that is often used in healthcare. It is used as a way to compare the quality of two or more health care providers. The 90th percentile is a measure of the quality of care that is provided by a health care provider. It is based on the performance of a particular provider compared to other providers in the same field.

The 90th percentile is calculated by taking the total number of patients served by a provider and dividing it by the total number of providers in the same field. The result is then multiplied by 100 to get the provider’s 90th percentile score. 

For example, if a health care provider has a total of 500 patients and there are a total of 1000 providers in the same field, then the provider’s 90th percentile score would be 50. This means that the provider is providing better care than 50% of the other providers in the field.
