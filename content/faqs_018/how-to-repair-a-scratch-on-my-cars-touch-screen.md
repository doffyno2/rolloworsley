<p>If you own a car with a touch screen, you know that it can be a major headache when it gets scratched. Not only does it look bad, but it can also make it difficult to use the screen if the scratch is deep enough. Fortunately, there are several ways to repair a scratch on your car’s touch screen. </p>

<h2>Step 1: Clean the Screen </h2>

<p>The first step in repairing a scratch on your car’s touch screen is to clean the screen. You can do this by using a soft, lint-free cloth and a mild glass cleaner. It’s important to be gentle when cleaning the screen, as harsh scrubbing can make the scratch worse. </p>

<h2>Step 2: Apply a Polishing Compound </h2>

<p>Once you’ve cleaned the screen, the next step is to apply a polishing compound. This is a special compound that is designed to fill in the scratches and make them less visible. You can buy a polishing compound at most auto supply stores. Be sure to follow the directions on the package when applying the compound. </p>

<h2>Step 3: Buff the Screen </h2>

<p>After you’ve applied the polishing compound, you’ll need to buff the screen. This can be done with a soft cloth and a mild glass cleaner. It’s important to be gentle, as scrubbing too hard can make the scratch worse. </p>

<h2>Step 4: Apply a Touch Screen Protector </h2>

<p>The final step in repairing a scratch on your car’s touch screen is to apply a touch screen protector. This is a special film that is designed to protect your screen from scratches and other damage. You can buy touch screen protectors at most electronics stores. Be sure to follow the instructions on the package when applying the protector. </p>
