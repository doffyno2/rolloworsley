<p>Salad spinners are a popular kitchen gadget for quickly and easily drying salad greens after washing. They are usually made of plastic and have a pump handle or crank that spins the inner container. This spinning motion creates centrifugal force and separates the water from the greens.</p>

<p>Progressive International is a well-known manufacturer of salad spinners. Their products are designed to be durable and reliable. However, over time, the plastic components of these appliances can wear out or break, leading to a need for repair. This article will discuss how to repair a Progressive International salad spinner.</p>

<h2>Tools and Materials Needed</h2>

<p>Before attempting to repair a Progressive International salad spinner, you'll need to gather the following tools and materials:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Replacement parts for the salad spinner</li>
  <li>Phillips head screwdriver</li>
  <li>Flathead screwdriver</li>
  <li>Pliers</li>
  <li>Duct tape</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps for Repairing a Progressive Salad Spinner</h2>

<p>Once you have all the necessary tools and materials, you can begin the repair process. The following steps should be followed to ensure successful repair of your Progressive International salad spinner:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Disassemble the Salad Spinner</h3>

<p>The first step in repairing your Progressive International salad spinner is to disassemble it. This can be done by unscrewing the screws at the base of the spinner, then carefully removing the lid and the inner basket. Be sure to keep track of all the screws and other small parts so that you don't lose them during the repair process.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Inspect and Replace Any Worn or Broken Parts</h3>

<p>Once the salad spinner is disassembled, you should inspect all the components for any signs of wear or damage. If any parts are worn or broken, you should replace them with new parts. Be sure to check the spinner's pump handle and crank for any signs of damage, as well as the plastic housing, plastic gears, and plastic tab that holds the lid in place.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Reassemble the Salad Spinner</h3>

<p>Once all the parts have been replaced or repaired, you can begin reassembling the salad spinner. This can be done by first replacing the plastic tab that holds the lid in place, then carefully replacing the inner basket and lid. Once these components are in place, you can screw the screws back into place at the base of the spinner.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Test the Salad Spinner</h3>

<p>Once you have reassembled the salad spinner, you should test it to make sure it is working properly. To test the spinner, simply fill it with a few handfuls of salad greens and pump or crank the handle. If the spinner is working correctly, the greens should be dried within a few minutes.</p>
