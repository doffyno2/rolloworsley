If you’re planning a trip, it’s important to know how much it will cost to get you there. Here’s a guide to help you calculate the cost of your journey. 

<h2>Distance and Travel Time</h2>

The distance from Fort Lauderdale to Orlando is about 200 miles, and the average drive time is 3-4 hours. This can vary depending on the route you take and how much traffic you encounter.

<h2>Gas Prices</h2>

The price of gas will have a major impact on the overall cost of your trip. If you’re traveling during peak times, such as summer, you may find that gas prices are higher than normal. Since you’re traveling a relatively short distance, you should be able to get by without having to pay too much for fuel. 

<h2>Tolls</h2>

Your route will likely include a few tolls along the way, which can add to the overall cost of your journey. Tolls in Florida range from $0.75 to $2.50 per toll plaza, so be sure to factor that into your budget. 

<h2>Car Rental Fees</h2>

If you don’t own a car or don’t want to use your own, you’ll need to rent one for your trip. Rental fees will vary depending on the type of car you choose, but you can expect to pay anywhere from $25 to $100 per day. You’ll also need to factor in the cost of insurance, which can add an additional fee to the cost of your rental. 

<h2>Parking Fees</h2>

If you’re staying in a hotel in either Fort Lauderdale or Orlando, you may need to pay for parking while you’re there. Most hotels charge a daily fee for parking, ranging from $5 to $20 per day. 

<h2>Total Cost of Trip</h2>
