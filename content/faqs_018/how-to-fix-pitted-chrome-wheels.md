<h2>What Causes Pitted Chrome Wheels?</h2>

Pitted chrome wheels are a common problem for many car owners. The cause of pitting is usually a combination of environmental factors, such as road salt, mud, and dirt, combined with the age of the wheel. Pitting occurs when metal is exposed to a combination of elements, such as heat, moisture, and oxygen, which can cause a reaction that causes the metal to corrode. The corrosion of the metal can cause small pits to form on the chrome.

<h2>How to Inspect a Chrome Wheel for Pitting</h2>

Inspecting a chrome wheel for pitting is a relatively simple process. First, use a flashlight to inspect around the wheel for any signs of pitting. When inspecting the wheel, look for any areas where the chrome is pitted or discolored, as this is a sign of pitting. If any areas of the wheel show signs of pitting, then it is likely that the wheel needs to be refinished or repaired.

<h2>How to Repair Pitted Chrome Wheels</h2>

The best way to repair pitted chrome wheels is to refinish them. Refinishing the wheel will remove any surface pitting and restore the wheel to its original condition. Many auto body shops offer wheel refinishing services, and the process typically involves sanding, buffing, and polishing the wheel. It is important to note that the wheel must be properly prepped for refinishing, which may include sanding the wheel to remove any oxidation.

<h2>How to Prevent Pitted Chrome Wheels</h2>

The best way to prevent pitted chrome wheels is to keep them clean and dry. Cleaning the wheels on a regular basis will help remove any dirt, mud, and road salt that can contribute to pitting. If the wheel is exposed to harsh elements, such as road salt, it is important to rinse the wheel off with water immediately after exposure. Additionally, keeping the wheel dry is important to prevent corrosion.
