Leather car seats can become damaged over time, and one of the most common types of damage is cigarette burns. Cigarette burns can occur when a cigarette is not properly extinguished before being set down on a leather car seat. Repairing a cigarette burn in leather car seats may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done in just a few easy steps.

<h2>What You'll Need to Repair a Cigarette Burn in Leather Car Seats</h2>

Before attempting to repair a cigarette burn in leather car seats, you will need to have the following tools and materials on hand:

<ul>
	<li>Leather cleaner</li>
	<li>Leather conditioner</li>
	<li>Leather patch</li>
	<li>Scissors</li>
	<li>Adhesive</li>
	<li>Soft bristled brush</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps for Repairing a Cigarette Burn in Leather Car Seats</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Clean the Burned Area</h3>

Using a soft bristled brush, gently scrub the burned area to remove any dirt or debris. Then, spray the area with leather cleaner and allow it to sit for a few minutes. Wipe off the cleaner with a clean, damp cloth and then dry the area thoroughly with a dry cloth.

<h3>Step 2: Apply Patch and Adhesive</h3>

Cut a piece of leather patch that is slightly larger than the burned area. Then, apply adhesive to the back of the patch and place it over the burned area. Gently press down on the patch to ensure it is secure.

<h3>Step 3: Allow the Patch to Dry</h3>

Allow the patch and adhesive to dry completely, which may take several hours.

<h3>Step 4: Apply Leather Conditioner</h3>

Once the patch has dried, apply a leather conditioner to the burned area in order to restore its natural shine and luster.
