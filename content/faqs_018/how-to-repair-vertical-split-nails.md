Fortunately, there are some simple steps you can take to repair vertical split nails, and this article will walk you through the process.</p>

<h2>What Causes Vertical Split Nails?</h2>

<p>Vertical split nails can be caused by a number of factors, including trauma, such as dropping something on your hands, or simply aging. As we age, our nails can become brittle, making them more susceptible to splitting. Other causes of vertical split nails can include fungal infections, psoriasis, or even a vitamin deficiency. In any case, it’s important to understand the cause of your vertical split nails in order to properly treat them.</p>

<h2>How to Repair Vertical Split Nails</h2>

<p>The first step to repairing vertical split nails is to clean the nail bed. This can be done by soaking the nail in warm water for several minutes. Then, use a nail brush to gently scrub the nail bed, taking care not to damage the nail bed or surrounding skin. After cleaning, pat the nail dry with a soft cloth.</p>

<p>Once the nail bed is clean, it’s time to apply a nail repair product. There are several products on the market specifically designed to repair vertical split nails, and it’s important to choose one that’s right for your particular nail type. Most nail repair products contain a combination of keratin, vitamins, and proteins, which help to strengthen and repair the nail.</p>

<p>After applying the nail repair product, it’s important to keep the nail bed as dry as possible. This can be done by wearing gloves when washing your hands, and avoiding activities that may introduce moisture to the nail bed, such as swimming or using hot tubs. It’s also important to keep the nail trimmed and filed to reduce the risk of further splitting.</p>

<p>Finally, it’s important to keep the nail bed hydrated. This can be done by applying a moisturizer or lotion to the nail bed several times a day. This will help to keep the nail bed hydrated and healthy, and will also help to prevent further splitting.</p>
