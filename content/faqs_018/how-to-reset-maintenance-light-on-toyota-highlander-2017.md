Resetting the Maintenance Required Light on the Toyota Highlander from 2017 model year is relatively simple and does not require any tools. With a few steps, you will be able to reset the maintenance light for your Toyota Highlander in no time.</p>

<h2>Steps to Reset Maintenance Light on Toyota Highlander 2017</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Turn on the Ignition</h3>

<p>The first step in resetting the Maintenance Required Light on the Toyota Highlander from 2017 model year is to turn on the ignition. Make sure all the accessories are turned off and that the vehicle is in Park.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Press and Hold the Trip Meter Reset Button</h3>

<p>Once the ignition is on, press and hold the trip meter reset button. This button is located on the instrument panel, just above the odometer. Hold the button down for a few seconds until the Maintenance Required Light begins to blink.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Release the Trip Meter Reset Button</h3>

<p>Once the light has started to blink, you can release the trip meter reset button. The Maintenance Required Light will continue to blink for a few seconds before it turns off, indicating that the reset is complete.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Turn off the Ignition</h3>

<p>The last step in resetting the Maintenance Required Light on the Toyota Highlander from 2017 model year is to turn off the ignition. This will complete the reset process and the Maintenance Required Light should no longer be illuminated.</p>
