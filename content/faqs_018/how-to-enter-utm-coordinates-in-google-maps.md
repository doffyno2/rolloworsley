<h2>What are UTM Coordinates?</h2>
<p>UTM stands for Universal Transverse Mercator and is a coordinate system used for mapping on a global scale. UTM coordinates are a set of two numbers that identify a specific location on the Earth’s surface. UTM coordinates are typically expressed as two numbers, separated by a comma, such as 17.75, -105.5. These coordinates can be used to accurately pinpoint locations on Google Maps.</p>

<h2>How to Enter UTM Coordinates in Google Maps</h2>
<p>Entering UTM coordinates in Google Maps is a relatively straightforward process. To start, open Google Maps and type in the UTM coordinates you want to search for. For example, if you wanted to search for the coordinates 17.75, -105.5, you would type “17.75, -105.5” into the search bar. Google Maps will then take you to the exact location of the coordinates.</p>

<h2>Using UTM Coordinates to Measure Distance</h2>
<p>In addition to using UTM coordinates to find specific locations, you can also use them to measure distances. To use this feature, enter two sets of UTM coordinates into Google Maps, separated by a dash. For example, if you wanted to measure the distance between 17.75, -105.5 and 18.75, -106.5, you would enter “17.75, -105.5 - 18.75, -106.5” into the search bar. Google Maps will then draw a line between the two points and give you the distance in miles or kilometers.</p>
