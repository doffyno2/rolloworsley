<p>The 2017 Ford Escape is a great car for people who want a reliable and efficient vehicle. But like any car, it needs regular maintenance to keep it running smoothly. One way to remind you of necessary maintenance is the maintenance light. This light will come on when it is time for regular maintenance, such as oil changes, air filter replacements, and other services. But when the light comes on, it can be confusing to know what to do next.</p>

<p>Fortunately, resetting the maintenance light on a 2017 Ford Escape is a relatively easy process. By following these steps, you can reset the maintenance light and continue to drive your car with confidence.</p>

<h2>Steps to Reset Maintenance Light on 2017 Ford Escape</h2>

<p><b>Step 1:</b> Turn off the car. Once the car is off, press and hold the odometer button on the dashboard.</p>

<p><b>Step 2:</b> Turn the ignition switch to the "On" position. Do not start the car. The maintenance light should now be illuminated.</p>

<p><b>Step 3:</b> Press and hold the odometer button until the maintenance light turns off. This process should take approximately five seconds.</p>

<p><b>Step 4:</b> Release the odometer button and turn the ignition switch back to the "Off" position.</p>

<p><b>Step 5:</b> Start the car and the maintenance light should now be reset.</p>

<p><b>Q: What is the maintenance light?</b><br>
