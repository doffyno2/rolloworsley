<p>A sagging aluminum screen door can be a frustrating problem, as it can make your home look unkempt and make it difficult to open and close. Fortunately, repairing a sagging aluminum screen door is relatively easy, and it can be done in just a few steps. <h2>Step 1: Check the Hinges</h2>

<p>The first step in repairing a sagging aluminum screen door is to check the hinges. Over time, hinges can become loose or worn, which can cause the door to sag. To check the hinges, open the door and then close it. If it sags when it is closed, then the hinges may need to be tightened or replaced. If the hinges appear to be in good condition, then the problem may be with the door itself.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Adjust the Striker Plate</h2>

<p>If the hinges are in good condition, then the next step is to adjust the striker plate. The striker plate is the metal plate located on the door frame that the door latch engages when the door is closed. If the striker plate is not in the proper position, then the door may not close properly, which can cause it to sag. To adjust the striker plate, simply loosen the screws holding it in place and then reposition it so that the door latch engages properly when the door is closed.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Replace the Pivot Hinge</h2>

<p>If adjusting the striker plate does not solve the problem, then the next step is to replace the pivot hinge. The pivot hinge is the hinge located at the top of the door that allows it to swing open and closed. If the pivot hinge is worn or damaged, then it can cause the door to sag. To replace the pivot hinge, simply remove the old hinge and then install the new one in its place.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Adjust the Door Frame</h2>

<p>If adjusting the striker plate and replacing the pivot hinge does not solve the problem, then the next step is to adjust the door frame. Over time, door frames can become warped or bent, which can cause the door to sag. To adjust the door frame, simply loosen the screws that hold it in place and then reposition it so that the door is level. Once the door frame is in the proper position, re-tighten the screws to secure it in place.</p>

<h2>Step 5: Replace the Screen Door</h2>

<p>If all of the above steps fail to solve the problem, then the last step is to replace the screen door. Screen doors are typically made of aluminum, which can become worn and damaged over time. If the screen has become worn or damaged, then it may need to be replaced. To replace the screen door, simply remove the old door and then install the new one in its place.</p>
